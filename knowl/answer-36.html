Answer1.1.7.63.1

  1. From the remainder theorem, \( \begin{aligned} P(x) \amp = (x − a)Q(x) + P(a)\\ \amp = (x − a)Q(x) + 0\\ \amp =(x − a)Q(x) \end{aligned} \)

  2. By definition of a factor, if \(x-a\) is a factor of \(P(x) \), then \(P(x) = (x − a)q(x)\), so \(P(x) =(x − a)q(x) + 0\). The uniqueness guaranteed in the remainder theorem tells us that \(P(a) = 0\).
