Horizontal Asymptotes

Suppose \(f(x) = \displaystyle{\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}}\) is a rational function, where the degree of \(P(x)\) is \(m\) and the degree of \(Q(x)\) is \(n\).

  1. If \(m\lt n\), the graph of \(f\) has a horizontal asymptote at \(y=0\).

  2. If \(m = n\), the graph of \(f\) has a horizontal asymptote at \(y = \frac{a}{b}\), where \(a\) is the lead coefficient of \(P(x)\) and \(b\) is the lead coefficient of \(Q(x)\).

  3. If \(m\gt n\), the graph of \(f\) does not have a horizontal asymptote.
