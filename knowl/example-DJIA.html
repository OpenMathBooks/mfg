Example1.2.2
  1. The coordinates of point \(P\) in Figure 1.2.1 are \((15, 2412)\). What do the coordinates tell you about the function \(f\)?
  2. If the DJIA was 1726 at noon on October 20, what can you say about the graph of \(f\)?
Solution
  1. The coordinates of point P tell us that \(f(15) = 2412\), so the DJIA was 2412 at noon on October 15.
  2. We can say that \(f(20) = 1726\), so the point \((20, 1726)\) lies on the graph of \(f\). This point is labeled \(Q\) in Figure 1.2.1.
