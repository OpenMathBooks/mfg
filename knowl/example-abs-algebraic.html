Example2.5.13

Solve the equation \(\abs{3x − 6} = 9\) algebraically.

Solution We write the piecewise definition of \(\abs{3x − 6}\).
\begin{equation*} \abs{3x-6} = \begin{cases} 3x-6 \amp \text{if } 3x-6\ge 0 \text{, or } x\ge 2\\ -(3x-6) \amp \text{if } 3x − 6\lt 0 \text{, or }x\lt 2 \end{cases} \end{equation*}
Thus, the absolute value equation \(\abs{3x − 6} = 9\) is equivalent to two regular equations:
\begin{equation*}3x − 6 =9 \text{ or } −(3x − 6) = 9\end{equation*}
or, by simplifying the second equation,
\begin{equation*}3x − 6 =9 \text{ or } 3x − 6 = −9\end{equation*}
Solving these two equations gives us the same solutions we found in Example 2.5.8, \(x = 5\) and \(−1\).
