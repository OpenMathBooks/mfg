Example1.5.16

Solve \(\abs{4x − 15} \lt 0.01\).

Solution

First, we solve the equation \(\abs{4x − 15} = 0.01\). There are two cases:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 4x − 15 \amp = 0.01 \amp \text{or} \amp\amp 4x − 15 \amp= −0.01 \\ 4x \amp = 15.01 \amp\amp\amp 4x \amp = 14.99 \\ x \amp = 3.7525 \amp\amp\amp x \amp= 3.7475 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Because the inequality symbol is \(\lt\), the solutions of the inequality are between these two values: \(3.7475 \lt x \lt 3.7525\). In interval notation, the solutions are \((3.7475, 3.7525)\).
