Example1.3.32

The function \(A = f (t)\) graphed in Figure 1.3.33 gives a person's blood alcohol level \(t\) hours after drinking a martini. Sketch a graph of \(g(t) = 2 f (t)\) and explain what it tells you.

Solution

To sketch a graph of \(g\), we stretch the graph of \(f\) vertically by a factor of \(2\), as shown in Figure 1.3.34. At each time \(t\), the person’s blood alcohol level is twice the value given by \(f\). The function \(g\) could represent a person's blood alcohol level \(t\) hours after drinking two martinis.
