Example1.5.2

Write each statement using absolute value notation. Illustrate the solutions on a number line.

  1. \(x\) is three units from the origin.
  2. \(p\) is two units from \(5\).
  3. \(a\) is within four units of \(−2\).
Solution

First, restate each statement in terms of distance.

  1. The distance between \(x\) and the origin is three units, or \(\abs{x} = 3\). Thus, \(x\) can be \(3\) or \(−3\). See Figure 1.5.3.
  2. The distance between \(p\) and \(5\) is two units, or \(\abs{p − 5} = 2\). If we count two units on either side of \(5\), we see that \(p\) can be \(3\) or \(7\). See Figure 1.5.4.
  3. The distance between \(a\) and \(−2\) is less than four units, or \(\abs{a − (−2)} \lt 4\), or \(\abs{a + 2} \lt 4\). Count four units on either side of \(−2\), to find \(−6\) and \(2\). Then \(a\) is between \(−6\) and \(2\), or \(−6 \lt a \lt 2\). See Figure 1.5.5.
