Example2.3.6

The function \(E = f (h)\) graphed in Figure 2.3.7 gives the amount of electrical power, in megawatts, drawn by a community from its local power plant as a function of time during a 24-hour period in 2002. Sketch a graph of \(y = f (h) + 300\) and interpret its meaning.

Solution

The graph of \(y = f (h) + 300\) is a vertical translation of the graph of \(f\), as shown in Figure 2.3.8.

At each hour of the day, or for each value of \(h\), the \(y\)-coordinate is 300 greater than on the graph of \(f\). So at each hour, the community is drawing 300 megawatts more power than in 2002.
