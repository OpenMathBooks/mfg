Example1.2.15

Solve \((2x + 1)^{3/4} = 27\).

Solution

Raise both sides of the equation to the reciprocal power, \(\frac{4}{3}\). \begin{align*} \left[(2x + 1)^{3/4}\right]^{4/3} \amp= 27^{4/3} \amp\amp \text{Apply the third law of exponents.} \\ 2x + 1 \amp = 81 \amp\amp \text{Solve as usual.} \\ x \amp = 40 \end{align*}
