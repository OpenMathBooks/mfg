Example1.2.32

Use the graph in Example 1.2.27 to solve the inequality

\begin{equation*}−2x^3 + x^2 + 16x \ge 15\end{equation*}

Solution We first locate all points on the graph that have \(y\)-coordinates greater than or equal to \(15\). The \(x\)-coordinates of these points are the solutions of the inequality. Figure 1.2.33 shows the points, and their \(x\)-coordinates as intervals on the \(x\)-axis. The solutions are \(x \le −3\) and \(1\le x \le 2.5\), or in interval notation, \((-∞, −3] ∪ [1, 2.5]\).
