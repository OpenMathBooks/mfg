Example1.4.16

Compute the slope of the line that passes through the points \(P(−4, 2)\) and \(Q(5, −1)\) shown in Figure 1.4.17 Illustrate \(\Delta y\) and \(\Delta x\) on the graph.

Solution As we move from the point \(P(−4, 2)\) to the point \(Q(5, −1)\), we move \(3\) units down, so \(\Delta y = −3\). We then move \(9\) units to the right, so \(\Delta x = 9\). Thus, the slope is
\begin{equation*}m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\frac{-3}{9}=\frac{-1}{3}\end{equation*}
\(\Delta y\) and \(\Delta x\) are labeled on the graph.
