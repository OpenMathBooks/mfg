Example1.1.11

Solve \(\frac{3}{x − 2}= 4\).

Solution We begin by taking the reciprocal of both sides of the equation to get
\begin{equation*}\frac{x − 2}{3}= \frac{1}{4}\end{equation*}
We continue to undo the operations in reverse order. Multiply both sides by \(3\). \begin{align} x − 2 \amp = \frac{3}{4} \amp\amp \text{Add 2 to both sides.}\\ x \amp = 2 + \frac{3}{4}= \frac{11}{4} \amp\amp \frac{2}{1}=\frac{8}{4} \text{, so } \frac{2}{1}+ \frac{3}{4}=\frac{8}{4}+\frac{3}{4}=\frac{11}{4} \end{align} The solution is \(\frac{11}{4}\), or \(2.75\).
