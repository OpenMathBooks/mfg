Example1.4.8

Compute the slope of the line that passes through points \(A\) and \(B\) in FigureÂ 1.4.9.

Solution As we move along the line from \(A\) to \(B\), the \(y\)-coordinate changes by \(3\) units, and the \(x\)-coordinate changes by \(4\) units. The slope of the line is thus
\begin{equation*}\frac{\text{change in }y\text{-coordinate}}{\text{change in }x\text{-coordinate}}=\frac{3}{4}\end{equation*}
