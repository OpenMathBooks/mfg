Example1.1.13

The surface area of a sphere varies directly with the square of its radius. A balloon of radius \(5\) centimeters has surface area \(100\pi\) square centimeters, or about \(314\) square centimeters. Find a formula for the surface area of a sphere as a function of its radius.

Solution If \(S\) stands for the surface area of a sphere of radius \(r\), then
\begin{equation*}S = f (r ) = kr^2\end{equation*}
To find the constant of variation, \(k\), we substitute the values of \(S\) and \(r\). \begin{align*} 100\pi \amp = k(5)^2\\ 4\pi \amp= k \end{align*} Thus, \(S = f (r ) = 4Ï€r^2\).
