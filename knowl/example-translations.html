Example1.3.2

Graph the following functions.

  1. \(g(x) = \abs{x} + 3\)
  2. \(h(x) = \frac{1}{x}− 2\)

Solution
  1. The table shows that the \(y\)-values for \(g(x)\) are each three units greater than the corresponding y-values for the absolute value function. The graph of \(g(x) = \abs{x} + 3\) is a translation of the basic graph of \(y = \abs{x}\), shifted upward three units, as shown in Figure 1.3.3.
  2. The table shows that the \(y\)-values for \(h(x)\) are each two units smaller than the corresponding \(y\)-values for \(y = \frac{1}{x}\). The graph of \(h(x) = \frac{1}{x} − 2\) is a translation of the basic graph of \(y = \frac{1}{x}\), shifted downward two units, as shown in Figure 1.3.4.
