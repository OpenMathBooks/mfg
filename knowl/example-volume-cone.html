Example1.1.5

The formula \(V = \frac{1}{3} πr^2h\) gives the volume of a cone in terms of its height and radius. Solve the formula for \(r\) in terms of \(V\) and \(h\).

Solution Because the variable we want is squared, we use extraction of roots. First, multiply both sides by \(3\) to clear the fraction.
\begin{equation*} \begin{align*} 3V \amp = 3(\frac{1}{3} πr^2h)\\ 3V \amp = πr^2h \amp\amp \text{Divide both sides by } πh.\\ \frac{3V}{πh}\amp= r^2 \amp\amp \text{Take square roots.}\\ \pm\sqrt{\frac{3V}{πh}}\amp= r \end{align*} \end{equation*}
Because the radius of a cone must be a positive number, we use only the positive square root: \(r = \sqrt{\frac{3V}{πh}}\).
