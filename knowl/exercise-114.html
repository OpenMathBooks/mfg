  1. Sketch a parabola \(y = a(x − h)^2 + k\) and the line \(x = h\). We will show that the parabola is symmetric about the line \(x = h\).

  2. Label a point on the parabola with \(x\)-coordinate \(x = h + d\), where \(d\gt 0\). What is the \(y\)-coordinate of that point?

  3. Label the point on the parabola with \(x\)-coordinate \(x = h − d\). What is the \(y\)-coordinate of that point?

  4. Explain why your answers to parts (b) and (c) prove that the line \(x = h\) is the axis of symmetry for the graph of \(y = a(x − h)^2 + k\).

Answer

  2. See graph and (c)

  3. \(ad^2+k\)

  4. The two points on the parabola that are the same horizontal distance from the line \(x = h\) have the same \(y\)-coordinate, so they are symmetric about that line.
