Exercise55

  1. Is it true that \((x + y)^{−2} = x^{−2} + y^{−2}\)? Explain why or why not.

  2. Give a numerical example to support your answer.

Answer

  1. No, because \(\frac{1}{(x+y)^2}\) is not \(\frac{1}{x^2} + \frac{1}{y^2}\).

  2. Let \(x = 1\), \(y = 2\), then \((x + y)^{−2} = (1 + 2)^{−2} = 3^{−2} = \frac{1}{9}\), but \(x^{−2} + y^{−2} = 1^{−2} + 2^{−2} = 1 + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{4}\)
