A deep-sea diver is taking some readings at a depth of \(400\) feet. He begins rising at \(20\) feet per minute.

  1. Write an equation that expresses the diver’s altitude, \(h\), in terms of the number of minutes, \(m\), elapsed. (Consider a depth of \(400\) feet as an altitude of \(−400\) feet.)

  2. Find the intercepts and sketch the graph. (Suggestion: Scale the horizontal axis from \(0\) to \(24\) in increments of \(2\), and scale the vertical axis from \(−500\) to \(100\) in increments of \(50\).)

  3. What do the intercepts tell us about the diver's depth?
