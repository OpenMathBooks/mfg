\(h(t)=6^t \)

  1. \(y = -h(t) \)

  2. \(y = h(-t) \)

  3. \(y = -h(-t) \)

Answer

  1. \(y =-6^t\) Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((−\infty, 0)\), no \(x\)-intercept; \(y\)-intercept \((0, −1)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.

  2. \(y=6^{-t}\), Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((0, \infty)\), no \(x\)-intercept \((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, 1)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.

  3. \(y=-6^{-t}\), Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((-\infty, 0)\), no \(x\)-intercept \((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, -1)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.
