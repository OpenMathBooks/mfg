\(g(x)=2^x \)

  1. \(y = g(x-3) \)

  2. \(y = g(x-3)+4 \)

Answer

  1. \(y =-6^t\) Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((0, \infty)\), no \(x\)-intercept; \(y\)-intercept \((0, 0.125)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.

  2. \(y=2^{x-3}+4 \), Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((4, \infty)\), no \(x\)-intercept \((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, 4.125)\); horizontal asymptote \(y=4\)
