\(N(t)=\left(\dfrac{1}{2} \right)^t \)

  1. \(y = -N(t) \)

  2. \(y = 6-N(t) \)

Answer

  1. \(y =-6^t\) Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((-\infty, 0)\), no \(x\)-intercept; \(y\)-intercept \((0, -1)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.

  2. \(y=6-\left(\dfrac{1}{2} \right)^t \), Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((-\infty,6)\), \(x\)-intercept approximately \(-2.58\)\((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, 5)\); horizontal asymptote \(y=6\)
