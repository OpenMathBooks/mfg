The boiling point of water changes with altitude. At sea level, water boils at \(212\degree\)F, and the boiling point diminishes by approximately \(0.002\degree\)F for each \(1\)-foot increase in altitude.

  1. Write an equation for the boiling point, \(B\), in terms of \(a\), the altitude in feet.

  2. Complete the table of values.

  3. Choose scales for the axes and graph the equation.

  4. How much does the boiling point decrease when the altitude increases from \(1000\) to \(3000\) feet? Illustrate this amount on the graph.

  5. At what altitudes is the boiling point less than \(204\degree\)F? Illustrate on the graph.
