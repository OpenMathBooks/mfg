  1. Evaluate the function to complete the table.

  2. Sketch the graph of the function.

  3. The treadmill test began with walking at \(5.5\) kilometers per hour, then jogging, starting at \(12\) kilometers per hour and increasing to \(14\) kilometers per hour, and finished with a cool-down walking period. Identify each of these activities on the graph and describe the volunteer's heart rate during each phase.

Answer

  1. \(t\) \(3.5\) \(4\) \(8\) \(10\) \(15\)
    \(f(t)\) \(128\) \(154.75\) \(184.05\) \(150.93\) \(103.96\)

  3. The initial walking corresponds to the time interval from \(0\) to \(3\) minutes; the volunteer's heart rate was \(100\) beats per minute during the first \(3\) minutes. The jogging corresponds to the increasing portion of the graph, with the interval from \(3\) to \(4\) minutes, and an increase in the heart rate to about \(155\) beats per minutes. From \(4\) to \(9\) minutes, the volunteer maintained the higher speed, and the heart rate rose to about \(185\) beats per minute. The decreasing portion, from \(9\) to \(20\) minutes, corresponds to the cool down walking period; the heart rate at this decreased rapidly and leveled off to about \(100\) beats per minute.
