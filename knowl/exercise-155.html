Exercise11

The number of bacteria in a culture grows according to the function \begin{equation*}N(t) = N_0 e^{0.04t}\end{equation*} where \(N_0\) is the number of bacteria present at time \(t = 0\) and \(t\) is the time in hours.

  1. Write a growth law for a sample in which \(6000\) bacteria were present initially.

  2. Make a table of values for \(N(t)\) in \(5\)-hour intervals over the first \(30\) hours.

  3. Graph \(N(t) \).

  4. How many bacteria were present at \(t = 24\) hours?

  5. How much time must elapse (to the nearest tenth of an hour) for the original \(6000\) bacteria to increase to \(100,000\)?

Answer

  1. \(N(t)=6000e^{0.04t} \)

  2. \(t\) \(0\) \(5\) \(10\) \(15\) \(20\) \(25\) \(30\)
    \(N(t)\) \(6000\) \(7328\) \(8951\) \(10,933\) \(13,353\) \(16,310\) \(19,921\)

  4. \(15,670\)

  5. \(70.3\) hrs
