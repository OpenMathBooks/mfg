  1. Find \(S(0)\), \(S\left(\frac{1}{6}\right)\), and \(S(−1)\).

  2. Estimate the value of \(S\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)\) from the graph.

  3. For what value(s) of \(x\) is \(S(x) = −\frac{1}{2}\)?

  4. Find the maximum and minimum values of \(S(x)\).

  5. For what value(s) of \(x\) does \(S\) take on its maximum and minimum values?

Answer

  1. \(0, \dfrac{1}{2}, 0\)

  2. \(0.9\)

  3. \(\dfrac{-5}{6}\), \(\dfrac{-1}{6}\), \(\dfrac{7}{6}\), \(\dfrac{11}{6}\)

  4. Max: \(1\); Min: \(−1\)

  5. Max at \(x=−1.5, 0.5\); Min at \(x=−0.5, 1.5\)
