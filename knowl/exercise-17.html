Use the suggested row operations to form an equivalent matrix in upper triangular form. \begin{equation*} \left[ \begin{array}{@{}ccc|c@{}} 1 \amp -2 \amp 4 \amp 3 \\ 5 \amp -7 \amp 8 \amp 6\\ -2\amp 6 \amp -7 \amp 6\\ \end{array}\right] \longrightarrow \left[ \begin{array}{@{}ccc|c@{}} 1 \amp -2 \amp 4 \amp 3 \\ 0 \amp \text{?} \amp \text{?} \amp \text{?} \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp \text{?} \amp \text{?} \\ \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}

  1. Add \(−5 (\text{row }1)\) to \((\text{row }2)\text{.}\)

  2. Add \(2 (\text{row }1)\) to \((\text{row }3)\text{.}\)

  3. Multiply \((\text{row }2)\) by \(\frac{1}{3}\text{.}\)

  4. Add \(−2 (\text{row }2)\) to \((\text{row }3)\text{.}\)
