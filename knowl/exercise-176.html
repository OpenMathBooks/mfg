The graph shows the federal debt as a percentage of the gross domestic product (GDP), as a function of time, \(D = f (t)\). (Source: Office of Management and Budget)

  1. Evaluate \(f (1985)\) and explain its meaning.

  2. Solve \(f (t) = 70\) and explain its meaning.

  3. When did the federal debt reach its highest level since 1960, and what was that level? Write your answer with function notation.

  4. What is the longest time interval over which the federal debt was decreasing?

Answer

  1. \(f (1985) = 41\): The federal debt in \(1985\) was about \(41\%\) of the gross domestic product.

  2. \(t = 1942\) or \(t = 1955\): The federal debt was \(70\%\) of the gross domestic product in \(1942\) and \(1955\).

  3. In about \(1997\), the debt was about \(67\%\) of the gross domestic product, so \(f (1997)\approx 67.3\).

  4. The percentage basically dropped from 1946 to 1973, but there were small rises around 1950, 1954, 1958, and 1968, so the longest time interval was from 1958 to 1967.
