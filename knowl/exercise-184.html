The figure shows a graph of \(B = \dfrac{1}{3}p^3 − 3p + 2\).

  1. Solve \(\dfrac{1}{3}p^3 − 3p + 2 = 6\)

  2. Solve \(\dfrac{1}{3}p^3 − 3p + 2=5\)

  3. Solve \(\dfrac{1}{3}p^3 − 3p + 2\lt 1\)

  4. What range of values does \(B\) have for \(p\) between \(−2.5\) and \(0.5\)?

  5. For what values of \(p\) is \(B\) increasing?

Answer

  1. \(3.5\)

  2. \(−2.2, −1.2, 3.4\)

  3. \(p\lt −3.1\) or \(0.3\lt p\lt 2.8\)

  4. \(0.5\lt B\lt 5.5\)

  5. \(p\lt −1.7\) or \(p\gt 1.7\)
