The population of Citrus Valley was \(20,000\) in \(2000\). In \(2010\), it was \(35,000\).

  1. What is \(P_0\) if \(t = 0\) in \(2000\)?

  2. Use the population in \(2010\) to find the growth factor \(e^k\).

  3. Write a growth law of the form \(P(t) = P_0 e^{kt}\) for the population of Citrus Valley.

  4. If it continues at the same rate of growth, what will the population be in \(2030\)?

Answer

  1. \(20,000\)

  2. \(\left(\dfrac{35,000}{20,000} \right)^{1/10}\approx e^{0.056} \)

  3. \(P(t) = 20,000e^{0.056t} \)

  4. \(107,188\)
