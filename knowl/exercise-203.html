Exercise61

  1. Write \(\sqrt{x}\) with a fractional exponent.

  2. Write \(\displaystyle{\sqrt{\sqrt{x}}}\) with a fractional exponents.

  3. Use the laws of exponents to show that \(\displaystyle{\sqrt{\sqrt{x}}=\sqrt[4]{x}} \).

Answer

  1. \(x^{1/2}\)

  2. \(\left(x^{1/2} \right)^{1/2} \)

  3. \begin{align*} \sqrt{\sqrt{x}}\amp =\left(x^{1/2} \right)^{1/2} \amp\amp\text{By definition of fractional exponents.}\\ \amp=x^{1/4} \amp\amp\text{By the third law of exponents.}\\ \amp=\sqrt[4]{x} \amp\amp\text{By definition of fractional exponents.} \end{align*}
