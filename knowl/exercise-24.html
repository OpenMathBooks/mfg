Exercise16

The load, \(L\), that a beam can support varies directly with the square of its vertical thickness, \(h\). A beam that is \(4\) inches thick can support a load of \(2000\) pounds.

  1. Express \(L\) as a function of \(h\). Complete the table and graph your function in a suitable window.

  2. What size load can be supported by a beam that is \(6\) inches thick?

  3. How thick a beam is needed to support a load of \(100\) pounds?

  4. If you double the thickness of a beam, how will the load it can support change?
