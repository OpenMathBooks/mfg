Francine wants to make a rectangular box. In order to simplify construction and keep her costs down, she plans for the box to have a square base and a total surface area of \(96\) square centimeters. She would like to know the largest volume that such a box can have.

  1. If the square base has length \(x\) centimeters, show that the height of the box is \(h = \dfrac{24}{x}âˆ’ \dfrac{x}{2}\) centimeters. (Hint: The surface area of the box is the sum of the areas of the six sides of the box.)

  2. Write an expression for the volume, \(V\text{,}\) of the box as a function of the length, \(x\text{,}\) of its base.

  3. Complete the table showing the heights and volumes of the box for various base lengths.

    Explain why the values of \(h\) and \(V\) are negative when \(x=7\text{.}\)

  4. Graph your expression for volume \(V(x)\) in an appropriate window. Approximate the maximum possible volume for a box of surface area \(96\) square centimeters.

  5. What value of \(x\) gives the maximum volume?

  6. Graph the height, \(h(x)\text{,}\) in the same window with \(V(x)\text{.}\) What is the height of the box with greatest volume? (Find the height directly from your graph and verify by using the formula given for \(h(x)\text{.}\))

Answer

  1. The surface area is \(2x^2 + 4xh = 96\text{.}\) Solving for \(h\text{,}\) \(h=\dfrac{96-2x^2}{4x}=\dfrac{24}{x}-\dfrac{x}{2} \text{.}\)

  2. \(V=24x-\dfrac{1}{2}x^3\)

  3. If the base is more than \(7\) cm, the top and bottom alone exceed the total area allowed.

  4. \(\$4768.33\)

  5. \(4\) cm

  6. \(h=4\) cm
