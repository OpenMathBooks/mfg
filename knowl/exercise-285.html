  1. Refer to the Michaelis-Menten equation in Problem 49. Solve for \(\dfrac{1}{V} \text{,}\) then write your new equation in the form \(\dfrac{1}{V}= a\cdot \dfrac{1}{s}+b \text{.}\) Express \(a\) and \(b\) in terms of \(V\) and \(K\text{.}\)

  2. Use the data from part (c) of Problem 49 to make a table of values for \(\left(\dfrac{1}{s},\dfrac{1}{v} \right) \text{.}\)

  3. Plot the points \(\left(\dfrac{1}{s},\dfrac{1}{v} \right) \text{,}\) then use linear regression to find the line of best fit.

  4. Use your values for \(a\) and \(b\) to solve for \(V\) and \(K\text{.}\)

Answer

  1. \(\frac{1}{v}=\frac{K}{V}\cdot\frac{1}{s} +\frac{1}{V}\text{;}\) Therefore, \(a=\dfrac{K}{V} \) and \(b=\dfrac{1}{V} \)

  2. \(\frac{1}{s}\) \(3\) \(1.5\) \(1\) \(0.6\) \(0.4\) \(0.3\) \(0.15\)
    \(\frac{1}{v} \) \(12.5\) \(7.1\) \(5\) \(3.3\) \(2.6\) \(2.2\) \(1.7\)

  3. \(\dfrac{1}{v}=3.8\cdot \dfrac{1}{s}+1.1 \)

  4. \(V\approx 0.89, ~K\approx 3.37\)
