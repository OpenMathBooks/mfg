Exercise12

The table shows the productivity, \(P\), of a manufacturing plant as a function of the number of items produced, \(I\), and the hours of labor used, \(w\). Values of \(P\) are rounded to tenths where necessary.

  1. Choose one row of the table and decide if \(P\) varies directly with \(w\) or inversely with \(w\). Explain your method.

  2. Choose one column of the table and decide if \(P\) varies directly with \(I\) or inversely with \(I\). Explain your method.

  3. Find the constant of variation and write \(P\) as a function of \(I\) and \(w\). What are the units of \(E\)?

  4. Sketch a graph of \(P\) as a function of \(w\) for \(I = 500\), \(600\), \(800\), and \(1000\).

  5. Sketch a graph of \(P\) as a function of \(I\) for \(w = 100\), \(200\), \(300\), and \(400\).
