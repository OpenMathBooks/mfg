Exercise13

The water gushing out of a fire hose exerts a backward force that the firefighter must control. This force, called the nozzle reaction, \(R\), is a function of the diameter, \(d\), of the nozzle and the water pressure, \(P\), at the nozzle.

  1. Show that \(R\) varies directly with \(P\).

  2. Show that \(R\) varies directly with a power of \(d\). What is the power?

  3. Find the constant of variation and write a formula for \(R\) as a function of \(d\) and \(P\).

  4. A typical fire hose has nozzle diameter \(2\frac{1}{2}\) inches and nozzle pressure \(60\) psi. Use your formula to calculate the nozzle reaction.

Answer

  1. In each row, \(R = kp\) for some constant \(k\) that depends on the row.

  2. In each column, \(R = cd^2\) for some constant \(c\) that depends on the column.

  3. \(R = 1.57d^2 p\)

  4. \(588.75\) pounds: When we keep \(p\) constant and double \(d\), \(R\) is multiplied by a factor of \(4\). So the value at \(d = 2\frac{1}{2} \), \(p = 60\) should be \(4\) times the value at \(d = 1\frac{1}{4} \), \(p = 60\).
