A flock of eider ducks is making a \(150\)-mile flight at an average airspeed of \(50\) miles per hour against a moderate headwind.

  1. Express the ducks' travel time, \(t\text{,}\) as a function of the windspeed, \(v\text{,}\) and graph the function in the window \begin{align*} \small{\text{Xmin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Xmax}} = 50\\ \small{\text{Ymin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Ymax}} =20 \end{align*} (See Problem 1.4.6.1 of HomeworkÂ 1.4.6.)

  2. Write and solve an equation to find the windspeed if the flock makes its trip in \(4\) hours. Label the corresponding point on your graph.

Answer

  1. \(t=\dfrac{150}{50-v} \)

  2. \(4=\dfrac{150}{50-v} \text{;}\) \(v=12.5\) mph
