The formula \(F = \frac{9}{5}C + 32\) converts the temperature in degrees Celsius to degrees Fahrenheit.

  1. What is the Fahrenheit temperature when it is \(10\degree\) Celsius?

  2. What is the Celsius temperature when it is \(−4\degree\) Fahrenheit?

  3. Choose appropriate WINDOW settings and graph the equation \(y = \frac{9}{5}x + 32\).

  4. Find the slope and explain its meaning for this problem.

  5. Find the intercepts and explain their meanings for this problem.

Answer

  1. \(50\degree\)F

  2. \(−20\degree\)C

  4. The slope, \(\frac{9}{5} = 1.8\), tells us that Fahrenheit temperatures increase by \(1.8\degree\) for each increase of \(1\degree\) Celsius.

  5. \(C\)-intercept \(\left(−17\frac{7}{9}, 0\right)\): \(−17\frac{7}{9}\degree\) C is the same as \(0\degree\)F; \(F\)-intercept \((0, 32)\): \(0\degree\)C is the same as \(32\degree\)F.
