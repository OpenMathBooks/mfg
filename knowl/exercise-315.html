In England, oven cooking temperatures are often given as Gas Marks rather than degrees Fahrenheit. The table shows the equivalent oven temperatures for various Gas Marks.

  1. Plot the data and draw a line through the data points.

  2. Calculate the slope of your line. Estimate the \(y\)-intercept from the graph.

  3. Find an equation that gives the temperature in degrees Fahrenheit in terms of the Gas Mark.

Answer

  2. \(m = 25, ~b = 250\)

  3. \(y = 250 + 25x\)
