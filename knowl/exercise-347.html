  1. Draw a set of coordinate axes with a square grid (i.e., with units the same size in both directions). Sketch four lines through the point (0, 4) with the following slopes: \begin{equation*}m = 3, ~~~ m = −3,~~~ m = \frac{1}{3}, ~~~ m =\frac{−1}{3}\end{equation*}

  2. What do you notice about these lines?

Hint

Look for perpendicular lines.

Answer

  2. The lines with slope \(3\) and \(\frac{−1}{3}\) are perpendicular to each other, and the lines with slope \(−3\) and \(\frac{1}{3}\) are perpendicular to each other.
