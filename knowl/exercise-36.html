Find the intercepts of the graph for each equation.

  1. \(\displaystyle{\frac{x}{3}+\frac{y}{5}=1} \)

  2. \(\displaystyle{2x âˆ’ 4y = 1} \)

  3. \(\displaystyle{\frac{2x}{5}-\frac{2y}{3}=1} \)

  4. \(\displaystyle{\frac{x}{p}+\frac{y}{q}=1} \)

  5. Why is the equation \(\displaystyle{\frac{x}{a}+\frac{y}{b}=1} \) called the intercept form for a line?

Answer

  1. \((3,0), (0,5) \)

  2. \(\left(\dfrac{1}{2},0\right), \left(0,\dfrac{-1}{4}\right) \)

  3. \(\left(\dfrac{5}{2},0\right), \left(0,\dfrac{-3}{2}\right) \)

  4. \((p,0), (0,q) \)

  5. The value of \(a\) is the \(x\)-intercept, and the value of \(b\) is the \(y\)-intercept.
