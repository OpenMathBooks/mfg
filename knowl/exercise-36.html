  1. Explain why \(P(t) = 2\cdot 3^t\) and \(Q(t) = 6^t\) are not the same function.

  2. Complete the table of values for \(P\) and \(Q\), showing that their values are not the same.

Answer

  1. In the expression \(2\cdot 3^t\), only the \(3\) is raised to a power \(t\), and the result is doubled, but if both the \(2\) and the \(3\) were raised to the power \(t\), the result would be \(6^t\).

  2. \(t\) \(0\) \(1\) \(2\)
    \(P(t)\) \(2\) \(6\) \(18\)
    \(Q(t)\) \(1\) \(6\) \(36\)
