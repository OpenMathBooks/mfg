Exercise51

  1. Show that the graph of \(y=\sqrt{16-x^2} \) is a semicircle.

    Hint

    (Hint: Write the equation in the form \(x^2 + y^2 = r^2\). See Algebra Skills Refresher Section A.11 to review circles.)

  2. State the domain and range of the function.

  3. Graph the function in the window \begin{align*} \small{\text{Xmin}} \amp = −6 \amp\amp \small{\text{Xmax}} = 6 \\ \small{\text{Ymin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Ymax}} = 8 \end{align*} In what way is the calculator’s graph misleading?

Answer

  1. Squaring both sides of the equation gives the equation of the circle centered on the origin with radius \(4\), but the points in the third and fourth quadrants are extraneous solutions introduced by squaring. (The original equation allowed only \(y\ge 0\).)

  2. Domain: \([−4, 4]\); Range: \([0, 4]\)

  3. The calculator does not show the graph extending down to the \(x\)-axis.
