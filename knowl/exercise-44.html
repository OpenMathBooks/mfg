Exercise36

Let \(y = g(t) = ab^t\) be an exponential decay function, with \(a \gt 0\) and \(0\lt b\lt 1\).

  1. Suppose that the value of \(y\) is halved from \(t = 0\) to \(t = H\), so that \(g(H) = \frac{1}{2}\cdot g(0)\). Rewrite this fact as an equation in terms of \(a\), \(b\), and \(H\).

  2. What does your answer to (a) tell you about the value of \(b^H\)?

  3. Use the first law of exponents and your result from (b) to rewrite \(g(t + H)\) in terms of \(g(t)\).

  4. Explain why your result from (c) shows that the half-life is constant.
