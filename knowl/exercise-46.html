Exercise38

Let \(y = f(t) = ab^t\) be an exponential decay function, with \(a \gt 0\) and \( b\gt 1\). In this problem, we will show that there is a fixed value \(T\) such that \(y\) triples every \(T\) units.

  1. Suppose that \(f(T) = 3 \cdot f(0)\). Rewrite this fact as an equation in terms of \(a\), \(b\), and \(T\).

  2. What does your answer to (a) tell you about the value of \(b^T\)?

  3. Use the first law of exponents and your result from (b) to rewrite \(f(t + T)\) in terms of \(f(t)\).

  4. Explain why your result from (c) shows that an exponential decay function has a constant tripling time.
