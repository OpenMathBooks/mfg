The scatterplot shows weights (in pounds) and heights (in inches) for a team of distance runners.

  1. Use a straightedge to draw a line that fits the data.

  2. Use your line to predict the weight of a \(65\)-inch-tall runner and the weight of a \(71\)-inch-tall runner.

  3. Use your answers from part (b) to approximate the equation of a regression line.

  4. Use your answer to part (c) to predict the weight of a runner who is \(68\) inches tall.

  5. The points on the scatterplot are \((65.5, 130)\), \((66.5, 133)\), \((67, 135)\), \((69, 140)\), and \((70, 143)\). Use your calculator to find the least squares regression line.

  6. Use the regression line to predict the weight of a runner who is \(68\) inches tall.
