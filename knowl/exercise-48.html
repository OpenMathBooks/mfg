  1. Fill in the table, rounding your answers to three decimal places.

  2. Divide each natural logarithm in your table by \(\ln 2\). Explain the result.

Answer

  1. \(n\) \(2\) \(4\) \(8\) \(16\)
    \(\ln n\) \(0.693 \) \(1.386 \) \(2.079 \) \(2.773 \)

  2. Each quotient equals \(k\), where \(n = 2^k\). Because \(\ln n = \ln 2^k = k\cdot \ln 2\), \(k = \dfrac{\ln n}{\ln 2}\).
