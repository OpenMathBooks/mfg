  1. Write an equation for a parabola that has \(x\)-intercepts at \((2,0) \) and \((-3,0) \). What is the equation of the parabola's axis of symmetry?

  2. Write an equation for another parabola that has the same \(x\)-intercepts. What is the equation of the parabola's axis of symmetry?

Answer

  1. \(y=x^2+x-6\); \(~x=\dfrac{-1}{2} \)

  2. \(y=2x^2+2x-12\); \(~x=\dfrac{-1}{2} \)
