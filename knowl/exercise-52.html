Exercise45

  1. Show that the equation \(\dfrac{1}{y}-\dfrac{1}{x}=\dfrac{1}{k} \) is equivalent to \(y=\dfrac{kx}{x+k} \) on their common domain

  2. Graph the functions \(y=\dfrac{kx}{x+k} \) for \(k = 1, 2,\) and \(3\) in the window \begin{align*} \small{\text{Xmin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Xmax}} = 30 \\ \small{\text{Ymin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Ymax}} = 4 \end{align*} Describe the graphs.

Answer

  1. \(\dfrac{1}{y}=\dfrac{1}{x}+\dfrac{1}{k}=\dfrac{k+x}{xk}\text{,}\) so by taking reciprocals, \(y=\dfrac{kx}{x+k} \text{.}\)

  2. The graphs increase from the origin and approach a horizontal asymptote at \(y=k\text{.}\)
