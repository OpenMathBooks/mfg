Exercise47

  1. Graph the functions in the same window on your calculator: \begin{gather*} y = x^2 + 2x,~ y = x^2 + 4x,\\ y = x^2 + 6x,~ y = x^2 + 8x \end{gather*}

  2. Find the vertex of each graph in part (a) and plot the points.

  3. Find the equation of the curve in part (b).

  4. Show that the vertex of \(y = x^2 + 2kx\) lies on the curve for any value of \(k\).

Answer

  2. \((−1, −1), (−2, −4), (−3, −9), (−4, −16)\)

  3. \(y=-x^2\)

  4. The vertex of \(y = x^2 + 2kx\) is \((−k, −k^2)\)
