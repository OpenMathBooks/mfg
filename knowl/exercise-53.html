Exercise43

A rough estimate for the radius of the nucleus of an atom is provided by the formula \begin{equation*}r=kA^{1/3}\end{equation*} where \(A\) is the mass number of the nucleus and \(k\approx 1.3 × 10^{−13}\) centimeter.

  1. Estimate the radius of the nucleus of an atom of iodine-127, which has mass number \(127\). If the nucleus is roughly spherical, what is its volume?

  2. The nuclear mass of iodine-127 is \(2.1 \times 10^{−22}\) gram. What is the density of the nucleus? (Density is mass per unit volume.)

  3. Complete the table of values for the radii of various radioisotopes.

  4. Sketch a graph of \(r\) as a function of \(A\). (Use units of \(10^{−13}\) centimeter on the vertical axis.)

Answer

  1. \(6.5\times 10^{−13}\) cm; \(1.17\times 10^{−36} \text{ cm}^3\)

  2. \(1.8\times 10^{14} g/\text{cm}^3\)
