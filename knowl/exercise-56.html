The Michaelis-Menten equation is the rate equation for chemical reactions catalyzed by enzymes. The speed of the reaction \(v\) is a function of the initial concentration of the reactant \(s\) and is given by \begin{equation*}v = f (s) = \frac{Vs}{s+K}\end{equation*} where \(V\) is the maximum possible reaction rate and \(K\) is called the Michaelis constant. (Source: Holme and Peck, 1993)

  1. What value does \(v\) approach as \(s\) increases?

  2. What is the value of \(v\) when \(s = K\text{?}\)

  3. The table gives data from reactions of the enzyme D-amino acid oxidase.

    Plot the data and estimate the values of \(V\) and \(K\) from your graph.

  4. Graph the function \(v = \dfrac{0.88s}{s + 3.34}\) on top of your data points.

Answer

  1. \(V\)

  2. \(\dfrac{V}{2} \)

  3. \(V\approx0.7, ~K\approx 2.2\) (many answers are possible)
