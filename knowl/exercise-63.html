Exercise53

  1. Graph the functions \begin{equation*}y_1 = x^{1/2},~~ y_2 = x^{1/3},~~ y_3 = x^{1/4},~~ y_4 = x^{1/5}\end{equation*} in the window \begin{align*} \small{\text{Xmin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Xmax}} = 100 \\ \small{\text{Ymin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Ymax}} = 10 \end{align*} What do you observe?

  2. Use your graphs to evaluate \(100^{1/2}\), \(100^{1/3}\), \(100^{1/4}\), and \(100^{1/5}\).

  3. Use your calculator to evaluate \(100^{1/n}\) for \(n = 10\), \(n = 100\), and \(n = 1000\). What happens when \(n\) gets large?

Answer

  1. The graphs of \(x^{1/n}\) become closer and closer to horizontal when \(n\) increases (for \(x\gt 1\)).

  2. \(10,~ 4.64,~ 3.16,~ 2.51\)

  3. \(1.58, 1.05,~ 1.005\); the values decrease towards \(1\).
