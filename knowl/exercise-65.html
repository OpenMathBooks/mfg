The remainder theorem states, If \(P(x)\) is a polynomial and \(a\) is any real number, there is a unique polynomial \(Q(x)\) such that \begin{equation*}P(x) = (x − a)Q(x) + P(a)\end{equation*} Follow the steps below to prove the remainder theorem.

  1. State the division algorithm applied to the polynomials \(P(x)\) and \(x-a\).

  2. What must be the degree of \(r(x) \) in this case?

  3. Evaluate your expression from part (a) at \(x = a\). What does this tell you about the remainder, \(r (x)\)?

Answer

  1. If \(P(x)\) is a nonconstant polynomial with real coefficients and \(a\) is any real number, then there exist unique polynomials \(q(x)\) and \(r (x)\) such that \begin{equation*}P(x) = (x − a)q(x) + r (x)\end{equation*} where \(\text{deg }r (x)\lt \text{deg }(x − a)\).

  2. Zero

  3. \(P(a) = (a − a)q(a) + r (a) = r (a)\). Because \(\text{deg }r(x) = 0 \), \(r(x) \) is a constant. That constant value is \(P(a) \), so \(P(x) = (x − a)q(x) + P(a)\).
