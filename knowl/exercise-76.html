The total weight, \(S\text{,}\) that a beam can support is given in pounds by \begin{equation*}S = \frac{182.6wh^2}{l}\end{equation*} where \(w\) is the width of the beam in inches, \(h\) is its height in inches, and \(l\) is the length of the beam in feet. A beam over the doorway in an interior wall of a house must support \(1600\) pounds. If the beam is \(4\) inches wide and \(9\) inches tall, how long can it be?

Answer

\(37\) ft
