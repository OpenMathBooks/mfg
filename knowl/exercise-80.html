The cost, in thousands of dollars, for immunizing \(p\) percent of the residents of Emporia against a dangerous new disease is given by the function \begin{equation*}C(p) = \frac{72p}{100-p}\end{equation*} Write and solve an equation to determine what percent of the population can be immunized for \(\$168,000\text{.}\)

Answer

\(168=\dfrac{72p}{100-p}\text{;}\) \(p=70\%\)
