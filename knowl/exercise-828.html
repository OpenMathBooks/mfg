53

\(f (x) = \abs{x + 4} + \abs{x − 4}\)

  1. Using your graph, write a piecewise formula for \(f (x)\).

  2. Experiment by graphing \(g(x) = \abs{x + p} + \abs{x − q}\) for different positive values of \(p\) and \(q\). Make a conjecture about how the graph depends on \(p\) and \(q\).

  3. Write a piecewise formula for \(g(x) = \abs{x + p} + \abs{x − q}\).

Answer

  1. \( f(x) = \begin{cases} -2x, \amp x\lt -4 \\ 8, \amp -4\le x\le 4 \\ 2x, \amp x\gt 4 \end{cases} \)

  2. The graphs looks like like a trough. The middle horizontal section is \(y = p + q\) for \(−p \le x\le q\), the left side, \(x\lt −p\), has slope \(-2\) and the right side, \(x\gt q\), has slope \(2\).

  3. \( g(x) = \begin{cases} -2x+q-p, \amp x\lt -p \\ p+q, \amp -p\le x\le q \\ 2x+p-q, \amp x\gt q \end{cases} \)
