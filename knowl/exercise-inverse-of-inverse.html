Exercise1.29
  1. Find a formula for the inverse of the function \(f(x)=\frac{2}{x − 1}\)
  2. Show that \(f^{−1}\) undoes the effect of \(f\) on \(x = 3\).
  3. Show that \(f\) undoes the effect of \(f^{−1}\) on \(y = −2\).
Answer

  1. \(f^{−1}(y) = 1 + 2y\)

  2. \(f(3)=1 \), and \(f^{-1}(1)=3 \)

  3. \(f^{-1}(-2)=0 \) and \(f(0)=-2 \)
