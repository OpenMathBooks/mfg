Exercise 2.55
An evaporative cooler, or swamp cooler, is an energy-efficient type of air conditioner used in dry climates. A typical swamp cooler can reduce the temperature inside a house by 15 degrees. Figure 2.56a shows the graph of \(T = f (t)\), the temperature inside Kate’s house \(t\) hours after she turns on the swamp cooler. Write a formula in terms of \(f\) for the function \(g\) shown in Figure 2.56b and give a possible explanation of its meaning.
Answer

\(g(t) = f (t) + 10\). The outside temperature was \(10\degree\) hotter.
