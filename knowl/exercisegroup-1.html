Exercise Group

Use the properties of logarithms to expand each expression in terms of simpler logarithms. Assume that all variable expressions denote positive numbers.

7

  1. \(\log_b 2x \)

  2. \(\log_b\dfrac{x}{2} \)

Answer

  1. \(\log_b 2 + \log_b x\)

  2. \(\log_b 2 - \log_b x\)
8

  1. \(\log_b\dfrac{2x}{x-2} \)

  2. \(\log_b x(2x+3) \)

9

  1. \(\log_3 (3x^4) \)

  2. \(\log_5 1.1^{1/5} \)

Answer

  1. \(\log_3 3 + 4\log_3 x\)

  2. \(\dfrac{1}{t}\log_5 1.1 \)
10

  1. \(\log_b (4b)^t \)

  2. \(\log_2 5(2^x) \)

11

  1. \(\log_b \sqrt{bx} \)

  2. \(\log_3 \sqrt[3]{x^2+1} \)

Answer

  1. \(\dfrac{1}{2} + \dfrac{1}{2}\log_b x\)

  2. \(\dfrac{1}{3}\log_3 (x^2+1) \)
12

  1. \(\log_{10} \sqrt{\dfrac{2L}{R^2}} \)

  2. \(\log_{10} 2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{l}{g}} \)

13

  1. \(\log P_0 (1-m)^t \)

  2. \(\log_4 \left(1+\dfrac{r}{4}\right)^{4t} \)

Answer

  1. \(\log P_0 + t\log (1-m) \)

  2. \(4t [\log_4(4+r)-4t] \)
14

  2. \(\log \dfrac{a^3b^2}{(a+b)^{3/2}} \)
