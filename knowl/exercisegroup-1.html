Exercise Group

In Problems 1–8, use your calculator to graph each cubic polynomial.

  1. Describe the long-term behavior of each graph. How does this behavior compare to that of the basic cubic? How does the sign of the lead coefficient affect the graph?

  2. How many \(x\)-intercepts does each graph have? How many turning points? How many inflection points?

1

\(y = x^3 + 4\)

Answer

  1. The end behavior is the same as for the basic cubic because the lead coefficient is positive.

  2. There is one \(x\)-intercept, no turning points, one inflection point.
2

\(y = x^3 − 8\)

3

\(y = −2 − 0.05x^3\)

Answer

  1. The end behavior is opposite to the basic cubic (the graph starts in the upper left and extends to the lower right) because the lead coefficient is negative.

  2. There is one \(x\)-intercept, no turning points, one inflection point.
4

\(y = 5 − 0.02x^3\)

5

\(y = x^3 − 3x\)

Answer

  1. The end behavior is the same as for the basic cubic because the lead coefficient is positive.

  2. There are three \(x\)-intercepts, two turning points, one inflection point.
6

\(y = 9x − x^3\)

7

\(y = x^3 + 5x^2 − 4x − 20\)

Answer

  1. The end behavior is the same as for the basic cubic because the lead coefficient is positive.

  2. There are three \(x\)-intercepts, two turning points, one inflection point.
