In Problems 53–60, find the domain and range of each transformation of the given function.

53

\(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2} \)

  1. \(y=f(x-2) \)

  2. \(y=f(x)-2 \)

  3. \(y=f(x-3)-5 \)

Answer

  1. Domain: \(x \ne 2\); Range: \((0, \infty)\)

  2. Domain: \(x \ne 0\); Range: \((-2, \infty)\)

  3. Domain: \(x \ne 3\); Range: \((-5, \infty)\)
54

\(f(x)=\sqrt{x} \)

  1. \(y=-f(x) \)

  2. \(y=4+f(x) \)

  3. \(y=4-f(x) \)

55

\(f(x)=x^2 \)

  1. \(y=-2f(x) \)

  2. \(y=6-2f(x) \)

  3. \(y=6-2f(x+3) \)

Answer

  1. Domain: all real numbers; Range: \((-\infty,0)\)

  2. Domain: all real numbers; Range: \((-\infty,6)\)

  3. Domain: all real numbers; Range: \((-\infty,6)\)
56

\(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x} \)

  1. \(y=3f(x) \)

  2. \(y=3+f(x-1) \)

  3. \(y=3-f(x-1) \)

57

The domain of \(f\) is \([0, 10]\) and the range is \([−2, 2]\).

  1. \(y=f(x-3) \)

  2. \(y=3f(x) \)

  3. \(y=2f(x-5) \)

Answer

  1. Domain: \([3,13]\); Range: \([-2,2])\)

  2. Domain: \([0,10]\); Range: \([-6,6]\)

  3. Domain: \([5,15]\); Range: \([-4,4]\)
58

The domain of \(f\) is \([−4, 4]\) and the range is \([3, 10]\).

  1. \(y=f(x)+10 \)

  2. \(y=f(x+10) \)

  3. \(y=f(x-1)+4 \)

59

The domain of \(f\) is \((0,+\infty)\) and the range is \((0, 1)\).

  1. \(y=5f(x) \)

  2. \(y=3f(x+2) \)

  3. \(y=2f(x-3)+2 \)

Answer

  1. Domain: \((0,\infty)\); Range: \((0,5)\)

  2. Domain: \((-2,\infty)\); Range: \((0,3)\)

  3. Domain: \((3,\infty)\); Range: \((2,4)\)
60

  2. \(y=3f(x+1) \)
