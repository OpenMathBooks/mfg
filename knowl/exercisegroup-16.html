In Problems 1–8, use the graphs to answer the questions about the functions.

1

  1. Find \(h(−3)\), \(h(1)\), and \(h(3)\).

  2. For what value(s) of \(z\) is \(h(z) = 3\)?

  3. Find the intercepts of the graph. List the function values given by the intercepts.

  4. What is the maximum value of \(h(z)\)?

  5. For what value(s) of \(z\) does \(h\) take on its maximum value?

  6. On what intervals is the function increasing? Decreasing?

Answer

  1. June 21–24, June 29–July 3, July 8–14

  2. June 17–21, June 25–29, July 4–7

  3. June 22–24, June 27, June 29–July 4, July 8–14

  4. Avalanches occur when temperatures rise above freezing immediately after snowfall.

3

  1. Find \(R(1)\) and \(R(3)\).

  2. For what value(s) of \(p\) is \(R(p)=2\)?

  3. Find the intercepts of the graph. List the function values given by the intercepts.

  4. Find the maximum and minimum values of \(R(p)\).

  5. For what value(s) of \(p\) does \(R\) take on its maximum and minimum values?

  6. On what intervals is the function increasing? Decreasing?

Answer

  1. \(−1, 2\)

  2. \(3, −1.3\)

  3. \(R(−2) = 0\), \(R(2) = 0\), \(R(4) = 0\), \(R(0) = 4\)

  4. Max: \(4\); Min: \(−5\)

  5. Max at \(p = 0\); Min at \(p = 5\)

  6. Increasing: \((−3, 0)\) and \((1, 3)\); Decreasing: \((0, 1)\) and \((3, 5)\)

  1. Find \(F(−3)\), \(F(−2)\), and \(F(2)\).

Answer

  1. \(2, 2, 1\)

  2. \(−6 \le s \/t −4\) or \(0\le s\lt 2\)

  3. Max: \(2\); Min: \(−1\)

  4. Max for \(−3\le s\lt −1\) or \(3\le s\lt 5\); Min for \(−6\le s\lt −4\) or \(0\le s\lt 2\)
