Find the vertex and the \(x\)-intercepts (if there are any) of the graph. Then sketch the graph by hand.

3

  1. \(y = x^2 − 16\)

  2. \(y = 16- x^2\)

  3. \(y = 16x − x^2\)

  4. \(y = x^2 − 16x\)

Answer

  1. Vertex \((0, −16)\); \(x\)-intercepts \((\pm 4, 0)\)

  2. Vertex \((0, 16)\); \(x\)-intercepts \((\pm 4, 0)\)

  3. Vertex \((8, 64)\); \(x\)-intercepts \((0, 0)\) and \((16,0) \)

  4. Vertex \((8, −64)\); \(x\)-intercepts \((0, 0)\) and \((16,0) \)

4

  1. \(y = x^2-1 \)

  2. \(y = 1-x^2 \)

  3. \(y = x^2-x\)

  4. \(y = x-x^2\)

5

  1. \(y = 3x^2 +6x\)

  2. \(y = 3x^2-6x\)

  3. \(y = 3x^2+6\)

  4. \(y = 3x^2 − 6\)

Answer

  1. Vertex \((1, -3)\); \(x\)-intercepts \((0, 0)\) and \((-2,0) \)

  2. Vertex \((1, -3)\); \(x\)-intercepts \((0, 0)\) and \((2,0) \)

  3. Vertex \((0,6)\); no \(x\)-intercepts

  4. Vertex \((0,-6)\); \(x\)-intercepts \((\pm\sqrt{2}, 0)\)

6

  1. \(y = 12x-2x^2 \)

  2. \(y = 12-2x^2 \)

  3. \(y = 12+2x^2\)

  4. \(y = 12x+2x^2\)
