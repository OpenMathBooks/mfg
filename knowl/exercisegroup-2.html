5

Find values for \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) so that the graph of the parabola \(y = ax^2 + bx + c\) includes the points \((−1, 0)\), \((2, 12)\), and \((−2, 8)\).

Answer

\(a = 3, b = 1, c = −2\). The equation for the parabola is \(y = 3x^2 + x − 2\)

7

A survey to determine what percent of different age groups regularly use marijuana collected the following data.

  1. Use the percentages for ages \(15\), \(20\), and \(30\) to fit a quadratic equation to the data, \(P = ax^2 + bx + c\), where \(x\) represents age.

  2. What does your equation predict for the percentage of \(25\)-year-olds who use marijuana?

  3. Sketch the graph of your quadratic equation and the given data on the same axes.

Answer

  1. \(P = −0.16x^2 + 7.4x − 71\)

  2. \(14\%\). It predicts that \(14\%\) of the \(25\)-year old population use marijuana on a regular basis.

The data below show Americans' annual per capita consumption of chicken for several years since \(1985\).

  1. Use the values for \(1987\) through \(1989\) to fit a quadratic equation to the data, \(C = at^2 + bt + c\), where \(t\) is measured in years since \(1985\).

  2. What does your equation predict for per capita chicken consumption in \(1990\)?

  3. Sketch the graph of your equation and the given data.

Answer

  1. \(C = 0.75t^2 −1.85t + 56.2\)

  2. \(65.7\) lb

Answer
