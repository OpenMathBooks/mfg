For Problems 53–60, write a formula for each transformation of the given function and sketch its graph. State the domain and range of each transformation, its intercept(s), and any asymptotes.

53

\(f(x)=3^x \)

  1. \(y = f (x) − 4\)

  2. \(y = f (x − 4)\)

  3. \(y = −4 f (x)\)

Answer

  1. \(y = 3^x − 4\) Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((−4, \infty)\), \(x\)-intercept \((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, −3)\); horizontal asymptote \(y=−4\)

  2. \(y=3^{x-4}\), Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((0, \infty)\), no \(x\)-intercept \((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, 0.012)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.

  3. \(y=-4\cdot 3^{x}\), Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((-\infty, 0)\), no \(x\)-intercept \((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, -4)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.

  3. \(y=-6^{-t}\), Domain: \((-\infty, \infty)\); range: \((-\infty, 0)\), no \(x\)-intercept \((1.26, 0)\); \(y\)-intercept \((0, -1)\); the \(x\)-axis is the horizontal asymptote.
