  1. Use your calculator to graph each polynomial and locate the \(x\)-intercepts. Set \(\text{Xmin} =−4.7\), \(\text{Xmax} = 4.7\), and adjust Ymin and Ymax to get a good graph.

  2. Write the polynomial in factored form.

  3. Expand the factored form of the polynomial (that is, multiply the factors together). Do you get the original polynomial?
