29

  1. Find \(f (−2)\) and \(f (2)\).

  2. For what value(s) of \(t\) is \(f (t) = 4\)?

  3. Find the \(t\)- and \(f(t)\)-intercepts of the graph.

  4. What is the maximum value of \(f\)? For what value(s) of \(t\) does \(f\) take on its maximum value?

Answer

  1. \(f (−2) = 3, ~~f (2) = 5\)

  2. \(t = 1, ~~t = 3\)

  3. \(t\)-intercepts \((−3, 0), (4, 0)\); \(f (t)\)-intercept: \((0, 2)\)

  4. Maximum value of \(5\) occurs at \(t = 2\)
