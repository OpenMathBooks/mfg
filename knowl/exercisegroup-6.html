Suppose \(y\) varies directly with \(x\). Show that if you multiply \(x\) by any constant \(c\), then \(y\) will be multiplied by the same constant.

53

Explain why the ratio \(\frac{y}{x^2}\) is a constant when \(y\) varies directly with \(x^2\).

Answer

If \(y = kx^2\), then dividing both sides of the equation by \(x^2\) gives \(\frac{y}{x^2} = k\).
