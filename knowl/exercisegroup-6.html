57

  1. Suppose you invest \(\$1000\) at \(12\%\) annual interest for \(5\) years. In this problem, we will investigate how the number of compounding periods,\(n\), affects the amount, \(A\). Write \(A\) as a function of \(n\), with \(P = 1000\), \(r = 0.12\), and \(t = 5\).

  2. Use your calculator to make a table of values for \(A\) as a function of \(n\). What happens to \(A\) as \(n\) increases?

  3. What value of \(n\) is necessary to produce an amount \(A\gt 1818\)? To produce \(A\gt 1820\)? To produce \(A\gt 1822\)?

  4. Graph the function \(A(n)\) in the window \begin{align*} \small{\text{Xmin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Xmax}} = 52 \\ \small{\text{Ymin}} \amp = 1750 \amp\amp \small{\text{Ymax}} = 1850 \end{align*} Describe the graph: Is it increasing or decreasing? Concave up or down? Does it appear to have an asymptote? Give your best estimate for the asymptote.

Answer

  1. \(A=1000\left(1+\dfrac{0.12}{n} \right)^{5n} \)

  2. \(A\) increases.

  3. \(16\); \(31\); \(553\)

  4. Increasing, concave down, asymptotically approaching \(A\approx 1822.12\)
