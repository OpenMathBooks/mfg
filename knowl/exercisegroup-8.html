For Problems 53–56, write the formula for each function in parts (a) through (d) and graph with a calculator. Describe how the graph differs from the graph of \(y = f(x)\).

53

\(f (x) = x^3 − 4x\)

  1. \(y=f(x)+3 \)

  2. \(y=f(x)-5 \)

  3. \(y=f(x-2) \)

  4. \(y=f(x+3) \)

Answer

  1. \(y = x^3 − 4x + 3\); The graph of \(y = f (x)\) shifted \(3\) units up.

  2. \(y = x^3 − 4x -5\); The graph of \(y = f (x)\) shifted \(5\) units down.

  3. \(y = (x − 2)^3 − 4(x − 2)\); The graph of \(y = f (x)\) shifted \(2\) units right.

  4. \(y = (x +3)^3 − 4(x +3)\); The graph of \(y = f (x)\) shifted \(3\) units left.

  1. \(y=f(x)+6 \)

  2. \(y=f(x)-2 \)

  3. \(y=f(x-1) \)

  4. \(y=f(x+2) \)

Answer

  2. \(y = x^4 − 4x^2 − 2\); The graph of \(y = f (x)\) shifted \(2\) units down.

  3. \(y = (x − 1)^4 − 4(x − 1)^2\); The graph of \(y = f (x)\) shifted \(1\) unit right.

  4. \(y = (x +2)^4 − 4(x +2)^2\); The graph of \(y = f (x)\) shifted \(2\) units left.
