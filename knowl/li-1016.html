The graph of \(y = x^2 − 2\) is shifted downward by two units, compared to the basic parabola. The vertex is the point \((0, −2)\) and the \(x\)-intercepts are the solutions of the equation \begin{equation*}0 = x^2 − 2\end{equation*} or \(\sqrt{2}\) and \(-\sqrt{2}\).
