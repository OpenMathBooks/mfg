Item c
To solve \(x^2 − 4 \gt 0\), locate the points on the graph where \(y \gt 0\), or above the \(x\)-axis. Points with positive \(y\)-values correspond to two sections of the \(x\)-axis, labeled I and III on the figure. In section I, \(x \lt −2\), and in section III, \(x \gt 2\). Thus, the solution to the inequality \(x^2 − 4 \gt 0\) includes all values of \(x\) for which either \(x \lt −2\) or \(x \gt 2\).
