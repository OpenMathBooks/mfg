  • Joint Variation

    • We say that \(z\) varies jointly with \(x\) and \(y\) if \begin{equation*}z = kxy, ~~~k \ne 0\end{equation*}

    • We say that \(z\) varies directly with \(x\) and inversely with \(y\) if \begin{equation*}z=k\frac{x}{y}, ~~~k\ne 0, ~~ y\ne 0 \end{equation*}
