Item b
We see that the slope of the line is \(m = \frac{4}{3}\) and its \(y\)-intercept is \(b = −2\). We begin by plotting the \(y\)-intercept, \((0, −2)\). We then use the slope to find another point on the line. We have \begin{equation*}m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\frac{4}{3}\end{equation*} so starting at \((0, −2)\), we move \(4\) units in the \(y\)-direction and \(3\) units in the \(x\)-direction, to arrive at the point \((3, 2)\). Finally, we draw the line through these two points. (See 1.130.)
