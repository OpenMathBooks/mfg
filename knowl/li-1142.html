Item I.C

Cubic Bézier Curves: Drawing a Letter y

A cubic Bézier curve is defined by two endpoints, \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_4, y_4)\), and two control points, \((x_2, y_2)\) and \((x_3, y_3)\). \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} x \amp= f(t) = x_1\cdot (1 − t)^2 +3x_2\cdot t(1 − t)^2 +3x_3\cdot t^2(1 − t) + x_4\cdot t^3\\ y \amp = g(t) = y_1\cdot (1 − t)^2 +3y_2\cdot t(1 − t)^2 +3y_3\cdot t^2(1 − t) + y_4 \cdot t^3 \end{aligned} \end{equation*} for \(0\le t \le 1\).

  1. Find the functions \(f\) and \(g\) for the cubic Bézier curve defined by the endpoints \((4, 7)\) and \((−4,−5)\), and the control points \((3, 3)\) and \((0,−8)\). Simplify the formulas defining each function.

  2. Fill in the table of values and plot the curve.

  3. Connect the four given points in order using three line segments. How does the position of the control points affect the curve? Finish the letter y by including the linear Bézier curve you drew for step I.A.2 in Part 1.
