Item a
We have two data points of the form \((t, W)\), namely \((3, 188.6)\) and \((13, 258.3)\). We use the point-slope formula to fit a line through these two points. First, we compute the slope. \begin{equation*}\frac{\Delta W}{\Delta t}=\frac {258.3 − 188.6}{13 − 3}= 6.97\end{equation*} Next, we use the slope m = \(6.97\) and either of the two data points in the point-slope formula. \begin{align*} W \amp =W_1 + m(t − t_1) \\ W \amp = 188.6 + 6.97(t − 3) \\ W \amp = 167.69 + 6.97t \end{align*} Thus, \(W = f (t) = 167.69 + 6.97t\).
