  • To solve \(x^2 − 4 \lt 0\), find the points on the graph where \(y \lt 0\), that is, below the \(x\)-axis. These points have \(x\)-coordinates between \(−2\) and \(2\) (labeled section II on the figure). Thus, the solution to the inequality \(x^2 − 4 \lt 0\) is \(−2 \lt x \lt 2\).
