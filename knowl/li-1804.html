Step 4

The graph lies above the \(x\)-axis, and hence \(y \gt 0\), for \(x\)-values between the two \(x\)-intercepts, that is, for \(122.54 \lt x \lt 277.46\). Because we cannot produce a fraction of a parka, we restrict the interval to the closest whole number \(x\)-values included, namely \(123\) and \(277\). Thus, TrailGear can produce as few as \(123\) parkas or as many as \(277\) parkas per month to keep its profit above \($2000\).
