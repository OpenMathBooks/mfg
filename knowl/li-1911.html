What happens to the values of \(f (x)\) as \(x\) approaches zero? Extend your graph of \(f\) to reflect your answer.

As \(x\) approaches zero from the left (through negative values), the function values decrease toward \(-\infty\). As \(x\) approaches zero from the right (through positive values), the function values increase toward \(\infty\). The graph approaches but never touches the vertical line \(x = 0\) (the \(y\)-axis.)We say that the graph of \(f\) has a vertical asymptote at \(x = 0\).
