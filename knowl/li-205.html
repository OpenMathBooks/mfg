Item1.2.6.23.a

  • The formula begins with \(x^3\) and ends with \(y^3\). As you proceed from term to term, the exponents on \(x\) decrease while the exponents on \(y\) increase, and on each term the sum of the exponents is \(3\). The coefficients of the two middle terms are both \(3\).
