Factor the polynomial to obtain \begin{align*} f(x) \amp= x(x^2 + 6x + 9) \\ \amp= x(x + 3)(x + 3) \end{align*} By the factor theorem, the zeros of \(f\) are \(0\), \(−3\), and \(−3\). (We say that \(f\) has a zero of multiplicity two at \(−3\).) Because all of these are real numbers, all will appear as \(x\)-intercepts on the graph. Thus, the \(x\)-intercepts occur at \((0, 0)\) and at \((−3, 0)\).
