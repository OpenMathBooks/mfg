Item b
The graph of \(y = −x^2 + 4\) opens downward and is shifted \(4\) units up, compared to the basic parabola. Its vertex is the point \((0, 4)\). Its \(x\)-intercepts are the solutions of the equation \begin{equation*}0 = −x^2 + 4\end{equation*} or \(2\) and \(−2\). (See Figure 6.46.) You can verify both graphs with your graphing calculator.
