First, locate the two points on the graph where \(y = 0\). These points are \((−2, 0)\) and \((2, 0)\). Their \(x\)-coordinates, \(−2\) and \(2\), are the solutions of the equation \(x^2 − 4 = 0\). The two points divide the \(x\)-axis into three sections, which are labeled on the graph. On each of these sections, the value of \(x^2 − 4\) is either always positive or always negative.
