  • Find two numbers whose product is \(-12\) and whose sum is \(-1\text{.}\) Because the product is negative, the two numbers must be of opposite sign and their sum must be \(-1\text{.}\) By listing the possible factors of \(-12\text{,}\) we find that the two numbers are \(-4\) and \(3\text{.}\) Hence, \begin{equation*}x^2 − x − 12 = (x − 4) (x + 3)\end{equation*}
