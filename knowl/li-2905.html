One-half of \(5\) is \(\frac{5}{2}\), so the constant term is \(\left(\frac{5}{2}\right)^2\), or \(\frac{25}{4}\). Add \(\frac{25}{4}\) to obtain \begin{equation*}x^2 +5x \alert{{}+{}\frac{25}{4}}=\left(x +\frac{5}{2}\right)^2~~~~~~~  \underset{p^2 = (\frac{5}{2})^2 = \frac{25}{4}}{\stackrel {p = \frac{1}{2}(5) = \frac{5}{2}}{}}\end{equation*}
