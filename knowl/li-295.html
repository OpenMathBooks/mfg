The table shows that the \(y\)-values for \(h(x)\) are each two units smaller than the corresponding \(y\)-values for \(y = \frac{1}{x}\). The graph of \(h(x) = \frac{1}{x} − 2\) is a translation of the basic graph of \(y = \frac{1}{x}\), shifted downward two units, as shown in Figure 2.49.
