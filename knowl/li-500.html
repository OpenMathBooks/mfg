In the first region, \(x \ge 3\), we graph the line \(y = x − 3\). Because \(x = 3\) is included in this region, the endpoint of this portion of the graph, \((3, 0)\), is included, too. In the second region, \(x \lt 3\), we graph the line \(y = −x + 3\). Note that the two pieces of the graph meet at the point \((0, 3)\), as shown in Figure 2.45.
