Item b
We substitute the luminosity of Sirius, \(L = 23\), to get \begin{equation*}23 = M^4\end{equation*} To solve the equation for \(M\), we raise both sides to the \(\frac{1}{4}\) power. \begin{align*} (23)^{1/4} \amp = \left(M^4\right)^{1/4} \\ 2.1899 \amp = M \end{align*} The mass of Sirius is about \(2.2\) solar masses, or about \(2.2\) times the mass of the Sun.
