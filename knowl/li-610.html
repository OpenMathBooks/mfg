fundamental principle of fractions

n, the property that the value of a fraction is unchanged when both its numerator and denominator are multiplied by the same nonzero value. We express this formally by saying if \(a\) is any number, and \(b\) and \(c\) are nonzero numbers, then \(\displaystyle{\frac{a\cdot c}{b\cdot c}=\frac{a}{b}} \).
