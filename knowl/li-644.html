To find the height that gives a BMI of \(25\), we solve the equation \(25 = 319h^{−2}\). Note that the variable \(h\) appears in the denominator of a fraction, so we begin by clearing the denominator—in this case we multiply both sides of the equation by \(h^2\). \begin{align*} 25 \amp = \frac{319}{h^2} \amp\amp \text{Multiply both sides by }h^2.\\ 25h^2 \amp = 319 \amp\amp \text{Divide both sides by }25.\\ h^2 \amp = 12.76 \amp\amp \text{Extract square roots.}\\ h \amp\approx 3.57 \end{align*} To have a BMI under \(25\), Yarbrough would have to be over \(3.57\) meters, or \(11\) feet \(8\) inches tall. (In fact, he is \(6\) feet \(8\) inches tall.)
