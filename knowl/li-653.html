Item b
Let \(E(t)\) represent the company's sales assuming that sales will grow exponentially. Then \(E(t)\) has the form \(E(t) = E_0b^t\) . The percent increase in sales over the first year was \(r = 0.10\), so the growth rate is \begin{equation*}b = 1 + r = 1.10\end{equation*} The initial value, \(E_0\), is \(80,000\). Thus, \(E(t) = 80,000(1.10)^t\), and sales grow by being multiplied each year by \(1.10\). The expected sales total for the next year is \begin{equation*} E(2) = 80,000(1.10)^2= 96,800 \end{equation*} The values of \(E(t)\) for \(t=0\) to \(t=4\) are shown in the last column of Table 4.1.19.
