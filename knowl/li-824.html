multiplicity

n, (i) (of a zero of a polynomial) the number of times the corresponding linear factor appears as a factor of the polynomial. For example, \(-9\) is a zero of multiplicity one and \(7\) is a zero of multiplicity two for the polynomial \(p(x) =x^3 − 5x^2 − 77x + 441\) because \(p(x)\) factors as \(p(x) =(x + 9) (x − 7)^2\); (ii) (of a solution to a polynomial equation) the multiplicity of the zero of the corresponding polynomial. For example, \(−9\) is a solution of multiplicity one and \(7\) is a solution of multiplicity two for the polynomial equation \(x^3 = 5x^2 + 77x − 441\) because the equation can be written in the standard form \(p(x) = 0\), where \(p(x)\) factors as \(p(x) =(x + 9)(x − 7)^2\).
