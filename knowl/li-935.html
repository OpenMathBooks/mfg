Let \(A_S\) stand for the amplitude of the San Francisco earthquake. We are looking for the ratio \(A_S/A_N\). First, we will use the Richter formula to compute values for \(A_S\) and \(A_N\). \begin{equation*}6.9 = \log_{10}\left(\frac{A_N}{A_0}\right) ~~\text{ and }~~ 7.1 = \log_{10}\left(\frac{A_s}{A_0}\right) \end{equation*} Rewriting each equation in exponential form, we have \begin{equation*}\frac{A_N}{A_0}= 10^{6.9} ~~\text{ and }~~ \frac{A_S}{A_0}= 10^{7.1} \end{equation*} or \begin{equation*}A_N = 10^{6.9}A_0 ~~\text{ and }~~A_S = 10^{7.1}A_0\end{equation*} Now we can compute the ratio we want: \begin{equation*}\frac{A_S}{A_N}= \frac{10^{7.1}A_0}{10^{6.9}A_0}= 10^{0.2}\end{equation*} The San Francisco earthquake was \(10^{0.2}\), or approximately \(1.58\) times as powerful as the Northridge quake.
