Item1.4.8.35.a

  • Pressure is the force per unit area exerted by the balloon on the air inside, or \(P = \frac{F}{A}\). Because the balloon is spherical, its surface area, \(A\), is given by \(A = \pi d^2\). Because the force increases as the balloon expands, we will try a power function \(F = kd^p\), where \(k\) and \(p\) are constants, and see if this fits the data. Combine the three equations, \(P = \frac{F}{A}\), \(A = \pi d^2\), and \(F = kd^p\), to express \(P\) as a power function of \(d\).
