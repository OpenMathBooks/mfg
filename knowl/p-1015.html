In Example 5.1.13, we used a graph of \(h\) to read values of \(h^{−1}\). But we can also plot the graph of \(h^{−1}\) itself. Because \(C\) is the input variable for \(h^{−1}\), we plot \(C\) on the horizontal axis and \(F\) on the vertical axis. To find some points on the graph of \(h^{−1}\), we interchange the coordinates of points on the graph of \(h\). The graph of \(h^{−1}\) is shown in Figure 5.1.16.
