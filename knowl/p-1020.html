First, isolate the radical expression on one side of the equation. (This will make it easier to square both sides.) \begin{align*} \sqrt{x+2}\amp = x-4\amp\amp\text{Square both sides of the equation.} \\ \left(\sqrt{x+2}\right)^\alert{2}\amp = (x-4)^\alert{2} \\ x+2\amp = x^2-8x+16 \amp\amp\text{Subtract }x + 2 \text{ from both sides.} \\ x^2-9x+14\amp = 0\amp\amp\text{Factor the left side.} \\ (x-2)(x-7)\amp = 0 \amp\amp\text{Set each factor to zero.} \\ x=2\hphantom{000}\amp\text{or}\hphantom{000}x=7 \end{align*}
