Look again at the graph of \(g(x) = \displaystyle{\frac{x}{x + 1}}\) in Example 1.77. As \(\abs{x}\) gets large—that is, as we move away from the origin along the \(x\)-axis in either direction—the corresponding \(y\)-values get closer and closer to \(1\text{.}\). The graph approaches, but never coincides with, the line \(y=1\text{.}\). We say that the graph has a horizontal asymptote at \(y=1\text{.}\).
