The decibel scale, used to measure the loudness or intensity of a sound, is another example of a logarithmic scale. The loudness of a sound is measured in decibels, D, by \begin{equation*}D=10 \log_{10}\left(\frac{I}{10^{-12}}\right)\end{equation*} where \(I\) is the intensity of its sound waves (in watts per square meter).
