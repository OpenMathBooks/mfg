Scale both axes in intervals of 10 and then plot the two intercepts, \((60, 0)\) and \((0, −50)\). Draw the line through them, as shown in Figure 1.1.19. Now find another point and check that it lies on this line. We choose \(x = \alert{20}\) and solve for \(y\).
\begin{gather*} 150(\alert{20}) −180y = 9000\\ 3000 −180y = 9000\\ −180y = 6000\\ y =−33.\overline{3} \end{gather*}
Plot the point \((20, −33\frac{1}{3})\). Because this point lies on the line, we can be reasonably confident that our graph is correct.
