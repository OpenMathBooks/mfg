If the inverse of a function \(f\) is also a function, we often denote the inverse by the symbol \(f^{-1}\), read "\(f\) inverse."" This notation makes it clear that the two functions are related in a special way. For example, the function \(f (t) = 6 + 2t\) in Example 5.1.5 has inverse function \(f^{−1}(H) = \frac{H −6}{2}\).
