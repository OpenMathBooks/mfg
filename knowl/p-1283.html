How does the linear term, \(bx\), affect the graph? Let us begin by considering an example. Graph the equation \begin{equation*}y = 2x^2 + 8x\end{equation*} on your calculator. The graph is shown in Figure 6.3.10. Note that \(a = 2\) and that \(2 \gt 0\), so the parabola opens upward. We can find the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph by setting \(y\) equal to zero: \begin{align*} 0 \amp= 2x^2 + 8x\\ \amp= 2x(x + 4) \end{align*} The solutions of this equation are \(0\) and \(−4\), so the \(x\)-intercepts are the points \((0, 0)\) and \((−4, 0)\).
