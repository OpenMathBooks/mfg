As we move along the line from \(A\) to \(B\), the \(y\)-coordinate changes by \(3\) units, and the \(x\)-coordinate changes by \(4\) units. The slope of the line is thus \begin{equation*}\frac{\text{change in }y\text{-coordinate}}{\text{change in }x\text{-coordinate}}=\frac{3}{4}\end{equation*}
