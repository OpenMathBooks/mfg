Consider the quadratic equation \begin{equation*}y = 2(x − 3)^2 − 8\end{equation*} By expanding the squared expression and collecting like terms, we can rewrite the equation in standard form as \begin{equation*}\begin{aligned} y \amp= 2(x^2 − 6x + 9) − 8\\ y \amp = 2x^2 − 12x + 10 \end{aligned} \end{equation*} The vertex of this parabola is \begin{equation*}\begin{aligned} x_v \amp= \frac{-(-12)}{2(2)}=3\\ y_v \amp = 2(3)^2 − 12(3) + 10=-8 \end{aligned} \end{equation*} and its graph is shown in Figure 6.4.5.
