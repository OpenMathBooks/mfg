To understand why the vertex form works, substitute \(x_v = 4\) into \(y = −3(x − 4)^2 + 6\) from Example 6.4.6 to find \begin{equation*}y = −3(4 − 4)^2 + 6 = 6\end{equation*} which confirms that when \(x = 4\), \(y = 6\). Next, notice that if \(x\) is any number except \(4\), the expression \(−3(x − 4)^2\) is negative, so \(y \lt 6\). Therefore, \(6\) is the maximum value for \(y\) on the graph, so \((4, 6)\) is the high point or vertex.
