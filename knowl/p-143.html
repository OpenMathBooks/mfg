  1. If a quantity is multiplied by a constant factor, \(b\), in each time period, we say that it undergoes exponential growth or decay. The constant \(b\) is called the growth factor if \(b\gt 1\) and the decay factor if \(0 \lt b \lt 1\).

  2. Quantities that increase or decrease by a constant percent in each time period grow or decay exponentially.
