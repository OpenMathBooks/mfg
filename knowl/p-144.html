Because a polynomial function of degree \(n\) can have at most \(n\) linear factors of the form \((x − a)\), it follows that \(P\) can have at most \(n\) distinct zeros. Another way of saying this is that if \(P(x)\) is a polynomial of \(n\)th degree, the equation \(P(x) = 0\) can have at most \(n\) distinct solutions, some of which may be complex numbers.
