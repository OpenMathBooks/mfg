The appearance of the graph near an \(x\)-intercept is determined by the multiplicity of the zero there. For example, both real zeros of the polynomial \(g(x) = x^4 − 3x^2 − 4\) in Example 1.29b are of multiplicity one, and the graph crosses the \(x\)-axis at each intercept. However, the polynomial \(f(x) = x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x\) in Example 1.29a has a zero of multiplicity two at \(x = −3\). The graph of \(f\) just touches the \(x\)-axis and then reverses direction without crossing the axis.
