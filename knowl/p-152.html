To understand what happens in general, compare the graphs of the three polynomials in Figure 1.31.

  1. In Figure 1.31a, \(L(x) = x − 2\) has a zero of multiplicity one at \(x = 2\), and its graph crosses the \(x\)-axis there.

  2. In Figure 1.31b, \(Q(x) = (x − 2)^2\) has a zero of multiplicity two at \(x = 2\), and its graph touches the \(x\)-axis there but changes direction without crossing.

  3. In Figure 1.31c, \(C(x) = (x − 2)^3\) has a zero of multiplicity three at \(x = 2\). In this case, the graph makes an S-shaped curve at the intercept, like the graph of \(y = x^3\).
