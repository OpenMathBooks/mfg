You know that not all quadratic equations have real solutions. For example, the graph of \begin{equation*} f(x) = x^2 − 2x + 2 \end{equation*} has no \(x\)-intercepts (as shown in Figure 1.35), and the equation \begin{equation*} x^2 − 2x + 2 = 0 \end{equation*} has no real solutions.
