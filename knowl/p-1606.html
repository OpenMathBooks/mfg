The graph of a polynomial function depends first of all on its degree. We have already studied the graphs of polynomials of degrees \(0\), \(1\), and \(2\). A polynomial of degree \(0\) is a constant, and its graph is a horizontal line. An example of such a polynomial function is \(f(x) = 3\) (see Figure 7.2.1a). A polynomial of degree \(1\) is a linear function, and its graph is a straight line. The function \(f(x) = 2x − 3\) is an example of a polynomial of degree \(1\). (See Figure 7.2.1b.)
