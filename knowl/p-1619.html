A right triangle is a triangle in which one of the angles is a right angle, or \(90\degree\). Because the sum of the three angles in any triangle is \(180\degree\), this means that the other two angles in a right triangle must have a sum of \(180\degree-90\degree\), or \(90\degree\). For instance, if we know that one of the angles in a right triangle is \(37\degree\), then the remaining angle must be \(90\degree-37\degree\), or \(53\degree\), as shown in 1.124.
