  1. We solve the equation for \(y\) in terms of \(x\). \begin{align*} −3y &=6 − 4x\\ y &= \frac{6 − 4x}{-3} =\frac{6}{-3}+\frac{-4x}{-3}\\ y &= -2+\frac{4}{3}x \end{align*}
  2. We see that the slope of the line is \(m = \frac{4}{3}\) and its \(y\)-intercept is \(b = −2\). We begin by plotting the \(y\)-intercept, \((0, −2)\). We then use the slope to find another point on the line. We have \begin{equation*}m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\frac{4}{3}\end{equation*} so starting at \((0, −2)\), we move \(4\) units in the \(y\)-direction and \(3\) units in the \(x\)-direction, to arrive at the point \((3, 2)\). Finally, we draw the line through these two points. (See 1.130.)
