Log scales allow us to plot a wide range of values, but there is a trade-off. Equal increments on a log scale do not correspond to equal differences in value, as they do on a linear scale. You can see why in Figure 1.96: The difference between \(10^1\) and \(10^0\) is \(10 − 1 = 9\), but the difference between \(10^2\) and \(10^1\) is \(100 − 10 = 90\).

If we include tick marks for intermediate values on the log scale, they look like Figure 1.97.
