The denominator, \(x+1\), equals zero when \(x = −1\). Because the numerator does not equal zero when \(x = −1\), there is a vertical asymptote at \(x = −1\). The asymptote separates the graph into two pieces. Use the Table feature of your calculator to evaluate \(g(x)\) for several values of \(x\) on either side of the asymptote, as shown in Figure 7.4.15a. Plot the points found in this way; then connect the points on either side of the asymptote to obtain the graph shown in Figure 7.4.15b.
