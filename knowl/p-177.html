A car traveling at \(s\) miles per hour on a dry road surface requires approximately \(d\) feet to stop, where \(d\) is given by \begin{equation*}d = \dfrac{s^2}{24}+\dfrac{s}{2} \end{equation*}

  1. Make a table showing the stopping distance, \(d\), for speeds of \(10\), \(20\), \(\ldots\) , \(100\) miles per hour. (Use the Table feature of your calculator.)

  2. Graph the equation for \(d\) in terms of \(s\). Use your table values to help you choose appropriate window settings.

  3. Write and solve an equation to answer the question: If a car must be able to stop in \(50\) feet, what is the maximum safe speed it can travel? Verify your answer on your graph.
