The graph of \(t(x) = \displaystyle{\frac{x^2 + 1}{x − 1}}\) in Figure 7.4.17c does not have a horizontal asymptote, because the degree of the numerator is larger than the degree of the denominator. As \(\abs{x}\) increases, \(x^2 + 1\) grows much faster than \(x − 1\), so their ratio does not approach a constant value. The function values increase without bound.
