The numerator and denominator of the fraction are both second-degree polynomials, so the graph does have a horizontal asymptote. The lead coefficients of \(P(x)\) and \(Q(x)\) are \(2\) and \(1\), respectively, so the horizontal asymptote is \(y = \frac{2}{1}\), or \(y=2\). The function \(h\) does not have a vertical asymptote because the denominator, \(x^2 + 4\), is never equal to zero. The \(y\)-intercept of the graph is the point \((0, 0)\).
