The density, \(D\), of a planet varies directly with its mass, \(M\), and inversely with the cube of its radius, \(r\).

  1. Use the data for Earth to find the constant of variation, then write a formula for \(D\) as a function of \(M\) and \(r\).

  2. Calculate the densities of the other planets.

  3. The planets are composed of three broad categories of materials: rocky materials, icy materials (including water), and the materials that dominate the sun, namely hydrogen and helium. The density of rock varies from \(3000\) to \(8000 \text{ kg}/\text{m}^3\). Which of the planets could be composed mainly of rock?
