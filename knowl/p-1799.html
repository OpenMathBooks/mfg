Tomoko sells \(\frac{4800}{x} \) exercise machines each month if the price of a machine is \(x\) dollars. On the other hand, her supplier can manufacture \(2.5x+20\) machines if she charges \(x\) dollars apiece for them.

  1. Graph the demand function, \(D(x) = \frac{4800}{x}\text{,}\) and the supply function, \(S(x) = 2.5x+20\text{,}\) in the same window.

  2. Write and solve an equation to find the equilibrium price, that is, the price at which the supply equals the demand for exercise machines. Label this point on your graph.
