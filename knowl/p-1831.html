Tartaglia's method always works to solve the special cubic equation, even when \(u\) and \(v\) are not convenient values. We will show why in this project.

  1. Expand the expression \((a − b)^3 + 3ab(a − b)\) and complete the identity. \begin{equation*} (a−b)^3+3ab(a−b)=\underline{\hspace{6.818181818181818em}} \end{equation*}

  2. Your answer to part (a) is actually Tartaglia's special cubic in disguise. Substitute \(x = a − b\text{,}\) \(m = 3ab\text{,}\) and \(n = a^3 − b^3\) to see this. Therefore, if we can find numbers \(a\) and \(b\) that satisfy \begin{align*} 3ab \amp = m\\ a^3 − b^3 \amp = n \end{align*} then the solution to Tartaglia's cubic is \(x = a − b\text{.}\)

  3. Compare the system in part (b) to the system from Project 1.2, \begin{gather*} u − v = n\\ uv=\left(\frac{m}{3}\right)^3 \end{gather*} to show that \(u = a^3\) and \(v = b^3\text{.}\)

  4. Use your answer to part (c) to show that Tartaglia's value, \(x=\sqrt[3]{u}- \sqrt[3]{v} \text{,}\) is a solution of \(x^3 + mx = n\text{.}\)
