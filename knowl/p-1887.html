Because two straight lines do not always intersect at a single point, a \(2\times 2\) system of linear equations does not always have a unique solution. In fact, there are three possibilities, as illustrated in Figure 8.1.11.

  1. The graphs may be the same line, as shown in Figure 8.1.11a.

  2. The graphs may be parallel but distinct lines, as shown in Figure 8.1.11b.

  3. The graphs may intersect in one and only one point, as shown in Figure 8.1.11c.
