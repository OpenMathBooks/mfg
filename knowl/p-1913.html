  1. We can use ratios to compare quantities.

  2. The slope ratio, \(\dfrac{\text{change in y-coordinate}}{\text{change in }x\text{-coordinate}}\), measures the steepness of a line.

  3. Notation for slope: \(m = \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\), \(\Delta x \ne 0\).

  4. Formula for slope: \(m =\dfrac{y_2 − y_1}{x_2 − x_1}\), \(~~x_2\ne x_1 \)

  5. Formula for slope: \(m =\dfrac{f(x_2) − f(x_1)}{x_2 − x_1}\), \(~~x_2\ne x_1 \)

  6. Lines have constant slope.

  7. Slope is a scale factor that tells us how many units \(\Delta y\) increases for each unit increase in \(\Delta x\) as we move along the line.

  8. The slope gives us the rate of change.
