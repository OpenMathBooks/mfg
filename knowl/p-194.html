Because \(i\) is really a radical (remember that \(i =\sqrt{-1})\), we multiply the numerator and denominator of the quotient by \(\alert{i}\) to obtain \begin{align*} \frac{(3+4i)\cdot \alert{i}}{2i\cdot\alert{i}}\amp =\frac{3i+4i^2}{2i^2} \amp\amp\text{Apply the distributive law to the numerator.} \\ \amp=\frac{3i-4}{-2}\amp\amp\text{Recall that }i^2=-1. \end{align*} To write the quotient in the form \(a + bi\), we divide \(-2\) into each term of the numerator to get \begin{equation*} \frac{3i}{-2}-\frac{4}{-2}=\frac{-3}{2}i+2=2+\frac{-3}{2}i \end{equation*}
