The graph in Example 1.193 is called a scatterplot. The points on a scatterplot may or may not show some sort of pattern. Consider the three plots in Figure 1.199. In Figure 1.199a, the data points resemble a cloud of gnats; there is no apparent pattern to their locations. In Figure 1.199b, the data follow a generally decreasing trend, but certainly do not all lie on the same line. The points in Figure 1.199c are even more organized; they seem to lie very close to an imaginary line.
