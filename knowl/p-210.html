Let \(y = f (t) = ab^t\) be an exponential growth function, with \(a \gt 0\) and \(b\gt 1\).

  1. Suppose that the value of \(y\) doubles from \(t = 0\) to \(t = D\), so that \(f (D) = 2\cdot f (0)\). Rewrite this fact as an equation in terms of \(a\), \(b\), and \(D\).

  2. What does your answer to (a) tell you about the value of \(b^D\)?

  3. Use the first law of exponents and your result from (b) to rewrite \(f (t + D)\) in terms of \(f (t)\).

  4. Explain why your result from (c) shows that the doubling time is constant.
