Ivana is investing in the hotel business. She has bought two hotels, and she will expand her investments when her total profit from the two hotels exceeds \(\$10,000\). If we let \(x\) represent the profit from one hotel and let \(y\) represent the profit from the other, then Ivana will expand her investments when \begin{equation*} x + y \ge 10,000 \end{equation*} Notice that the equation \(x + y = 10,000\) is not appropriate to model our situation, since Ivana will be delighted if her profits are not exactly equal to \(\$10,000\) but actually exceed that amount.
