Scientists and engineers regularly encounter very large numbers such as \begin{equation*}5,980,000,000,000,000,000,000,000\end{equation*} (the mass of the Earth in kilograms) and very small numbers such as \begin{equation*}0.000 \,000 \,000 \,000 \,000 \,000 \,000 \,001 \,67\end{equation*} (the mass of a hydrogen atom in grams). These numbers can be written in a more compact and useful form by using powers of \(10\).
