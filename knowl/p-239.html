A polynomial function has the form \begin{equation*}f (x) = a_n x^n + a_{n−1}x^{n−1} + a_{n−2}x^{n−2} + \cdots + a_2x^2 + a_1x + a_0\end{equation*} where \(a_0\), \(a_1\), \(a_2\), \(\ldots\), \(a_n\) are constants and \(a_n \ne 0\). The coefficient \(a_n\) of the highest power term is called the lead coefficient.
