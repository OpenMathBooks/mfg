You can use your graphing calculator to solve the problem in Example 8.5.13. Set the window values at \begin{align*} \text{Xmin} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \text{Xmax} = 9.4 \\ \text{Ymin} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \text{Ymax} = 6.2 \end{align*} Next, graph the set of feasible solutions. We have already taken care of the constraints \(x\ge 0\) and \(y\ge 0\) by setting \(Xmin\) and \(Ymin\) to zero. Solve each of the other constraints for \(y\) to get \begin{align*} y \amp\ge (12 − 2x)/3\\ y \amp\ge(9 − x)/3 \end{align*} For each constraint, the set of feasible solutions lies above the boundary line, because \(y\) is greater than the expression in \(x\). To shade the regions above the graphs of \(Y_1\) and \(Y_2\), move the cursor onto the backslash in front of the equations and press ENTER twice, as shown in Figure 8.5.17a. Then press GRAPH. Your display should look like Figure 8.5.17b.
