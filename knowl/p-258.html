Press , clear any old functions, and enter \begin{equation*}Y_1 = 5−X \text{^} 3\end{equation*} Then press TblSet (,2nd WINDOW) and choose Ask after Indpnt, as shown in Figure 1.2.25, and press ENTER. This setting allows you to enter any \(x\)-values you like. Next, press TABLE (using 2nd GRAPH). To follow Exercise 1.2.23, key in (-) 2 ENTER for the \(x\)-value, and the calculator will fill in the \(y\)-value. Continue by entering 0, 1, 3, or any other \(x\)-values you choose. One such table is shown in Figure 1.2.26. If you would like to evaluate a new function, you do not have to return to the Y= screen. Use the → and ↑ arrow keys to highlight \(Y_1\) at the top of the second column. The definition of \(Y_1\) will appear at the bottom of the display, as shown in Figure 1.2.26. You can key in a new definition here, and the second column will be updated automatically to show the \(y\)-values of the new function.
