The scatterplot shows best times for various women running 400 meters and 100 meters.

  1. Use a straightedge to draw a line that fits the data.

  2. Use your line to predict the 400-meter time of a woman who runs the 100-meter dash in \(11.2\) seconds and the 400-meter time of a woman who runs the 100-meter dash in \(13.2\) seconds.

  3. Use your answers from part (b) to approximate the equation of a regression line.

  4. Use your answer to part (c) to predict the 400-meter time of a woman who runs the 100-meter dash in \(12.1\) seconds.

  5. The points on the scatterplot are \((11.1, 52.4)\), \((11.5, 54.7)\), \((11.9, 57.4)\), \((12.2, 57.9)\), \((12.7, 61.3)\), and \((13.0, 63.0)\). Use your calculator to find the least squares regression line.

  6. Use the regression line to predict the 400-meter time of a woman who runs the 100-meter dash in \(12.1\) seconds.
