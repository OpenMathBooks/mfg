Look again at the graph of \(g(x) = \displaystyle{\frac{x}{x + 1}}\) in Example 1.76. As \(\abs{x}\) gets large—that is, as we move away from the origin along the \(x\)-axis in either direction—the corresponding \(y\)-values get closer and closer to \(1\). The graph approaches, but never coincides with, the line \(y=1\). We say that the graph has a horizontal asymptote at \(y=1\).
