Solve the inequality for \(y\text{.}\). \begin{align*} 4x − 3y \amp\ge 12 \\ −3y\amp\ge −4x + 12 \\ y\amp\le \frac{4}{3}x-4 \end{align*} Graph the corresponding line \begin{equation*} y = \frac{4}{3}x-4 \end{equation*} Note that the \(y\)-intercept is \(-4\) and the slope is \(\frac{4}{3}\text{.}\).
