Begin by locating the point \(P\) on the graph for which \(y = 150\), as shown in Figure 1.3.22. Now find the \(x\)-coordinate of point \(P\) by drawing an imaginary line from \(P\) straight down to the \(x\)-axis. The \(x\)-coordinate of \(P\) is \(x = 9\). Thus, \(P\) is the point \((9,150)\), and \(x = 9\) when \(y = 150\). The solution of the equation \(150 = 285 − 15x\) is \(x = 9\).
