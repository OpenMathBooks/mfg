\begin{equation*}−2x^3 + x^2 + 16x = 15\end{equation*} From the graph, we see that the solutions are \(x = −3\), \(x = 1\), and approximately \(x = 2.5\). We can verify the solutions algebraically. For example, if \(x = \alert{−3}\), we have \begin{align*} f(\alert{−3})&−2(\alert{−3})3 + (\alert{−3})^2 + 16(\alert{−3})\\ &= −2(−27) + 9 − 48\\ &=54 + 9 − 48 = 15 \end{align*} so \(−3\) is a solution.
