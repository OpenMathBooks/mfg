The ratio we computed in Example 1.4.4, \begin{equation*}\frac{\text{change in elevation}}{\text{change in horizontal position}}\end{equation*} appears on the graphs in Figure 1.4.6 as \begin{equation*}\frac{\text{change in }y\text{-coordinate}}{\text{change in }x\text{-coordinate}}\end{equation*} For example, as we travel along the line representing Stony Point trail, we move from the point\(0, 0)\) to the point \((2500, 400)\). The \(y\)-coordinate changes by \(400\) and the \(x\)-coordinate changes by \(2500\), giving the ratio \(0.16\) that we found in Example 1.4.42. We call this ratio the slope of the line.
