  1. A negative exponent does not mean that the power is negative! For example, \begin{equation*}2^{−3}\ne −2^3\end{equation*}

  2. In Example 1.29b, note that \begin{equation*}9x^{−2} \ne = \frac{1}{9x^2}\end{equation*} The exponent, \(−2\), applies only to the base \(x\), not to \(9\).
