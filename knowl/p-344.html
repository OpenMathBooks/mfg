Now consider a specific example. The line through the two points \(P_1 (2, 9)\) and \(P_2 (7,−6)\) is shown in Figure 1.4.26. We can find \(\Delta x\) by subtracting the \(x\)-coordinates of the points:
\begin{equation*}\Delta x = 7 − 2 = 5 \end{equation*}
In general, we have
\begin{equation*}\Delta x = x_2 − x_1 \end{equation*}
and similarly
\begin{equation*}\Delta y = y_2 − y_1 \end{equation*}
