The slope of a line is a ratio and can be written in many equivalent ways. In Example 1.5.10, the slope is equal to \(\frac{8}{6}\), \(\frac{12}{9}\), and \(\frac{−4}{−3}\). We can use any of these fractions to locate a third point on the line as a check. If we use \(m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}= \frac{−4}{−3}\), we move down \(4\) units and left \(3\) units from the \(y\)-intercept to find the point \((−3, −6)\) on the line.
