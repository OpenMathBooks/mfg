When working with rational functions, we often need to solve equations that involve algebraic fractions. In Example 1.1 of Section 1.1, we wrote a function that gave the time Francine needs for a 60-mile training run on her cycle-plane in terms of the windspeed, \(x\text{:}\): \begin{equation*} t=f (x) = \frac{60}{15-x} \end{equation*}
