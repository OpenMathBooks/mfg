Find two numbers whose product is \(12\) and whose sum is \(-7\). Because the product is positive and the sum is negative, the two numbers must both be negative. The possible factors of \(12\) are \(-1\) and \(-12\), \(-2\) and \(-6\), or \(-3\) and \(-4\). Only \(-4\) and \(-3\) have the correct sum, \(-7\). Hence, \begin{equation*}x^2 − 7x + 12 = (x − 4) (x − 3)\end{equation*}
