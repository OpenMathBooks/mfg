The line crosses the \(y\)-axis at the point \((0, 3200)\), so the \(y\)-intercept is \(3200\). To calculate the slope of the line, locate another point, say \((20, 6000)\), and compute: \begin{align*}m &= \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{6000 − 3200}{20 − 0}\\ &=\frac{2800}{20}= 140 \end{align*} The slope-intercept form of the equation, with \(m = 140\) and \(b = 3200\), is \(y = 3200 + 140x\).
