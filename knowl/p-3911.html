We can graph \(f(x)\) by applying transformations to the basic parabola, \(y = x^2\). To identify the transformations, think of evaluating the function for a specific value of \(x\). What operations would we perform on \(x\), besides squaring?
1. Subtract 4: \(y = (x \, \alert{-\,4})^2\) Shift 4 units right.
2. Multiply by −3: \(y = \alert{-3}(x -4)^2\) Stretch by a factor of 3,
and reflect about the \(x\)-axis.
3. Add 6: \( y = −3(x − 4)^2 \,\alert{+\,6}\) Shift up 6 units.
We perform the same transformations on the graph of \(y = x^2\), as shown in the figure.
