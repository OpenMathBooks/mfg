The graph of \(s(x) = \displaystyle{\frac{x^2 − 4}{2x^2}}\) in Figure 7.79b has a horizontal asymptote at \(y = \frac{1}{2}\), because the numerator and denominator of the fraction have the same degree. For large values of \(\abs{x}\), the terms of lower degree are negligible compared to the squared terms. As \(x\) increases, \(s(x)\) is approximately equal to \(\frac{x^2}{2x^2}\), or \(\frac{1}{2}\). Thus, the function values approach a constant value of \(\frac{1}{2}\).
