First, we write \(\sqrt{xy}\) using a fractional exponent: \begin{equation*}\log_{b}{xy} = \log_{b}{(xy)^{1/2}}\end{equation*} Then we apply Property (3) to rewrite the exponent as a coefficient: \begin{equation*}\log{b}{(xy)^{1/2}} = \frac{1}{2}\log_{b}{(xy)}\end{equation*} Finally, by Property (1) we write the log of a product as a sum of logs: \begin{equation*}\frac{1}{2}(\log_{b}{xy}) = \frac{1}{2}(\log_{b}{x} + \log_{b}{y})\end{equation*} Thus, \(\log_{b}\sqrt{xy} = \frac{1}{2}(\log_{b}{x} + \log_{b}{y})\).
