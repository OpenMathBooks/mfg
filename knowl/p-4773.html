Examples of power functions are \begin{equation*}V(r ) = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 ~~\text{ and }~~L(T ) = 0.8125T^2\end{equation*} In addition, the basic functions \begin{equation*}f (x) = \frac{1}{x} ~~\text{ and }~~ g(x) = \frac{1}{x^2}\end{equation*} which we studied in Chapter 2 can be written as \begin{equation*}f (x) = x^{−1} ~~\text{ and }~~ g(x) = x^{−2} \end{equation*} Their graphs are shown in Figure 3.8. Note that the domains of power functions with negative exponents do not include zero.
