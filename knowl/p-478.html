The graphs of the eight basic functions considered in Investigation 4 are shown in Figure 2.2.19, Figure 2.2.20, and Figure 2.2.21. Once you know the shape of each graph, you can sketch an accurate picture by plotting a few guidepoints and drawing the curve through those points. Usually, points (or vertical asymptotes!) at \(x = −1\), \(0\), and \(1\) make good guidepoints.
