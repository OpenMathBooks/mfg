Repeating the process, divide \(-5x\) by \(x\) to obtain \(-5\). Write \(-5\) as the second term of the quotient. Then multiply \(x+3\) by \(-5\) to obtain \(−5x − 15\), and subtract: Because the degree of \(8\) is less than the degree of \(x + 3\), the division is finished. The quotient is \(2x − 5\), with a remainder of \(8\). We write the remainder as a fraction to obtain \begin{equation*} \frac{2x^2 + x − 7}{x + 3}= 2x − 5 + \frac{8}{x + 3} \end{equation*}
