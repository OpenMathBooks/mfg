\begin{gather*} \text{Number of packages purchased from Company A: } ~x\\ \text{Number of packages purchased from Company B: } ~y \end{gather*} First write the objective function. The store would like to minimize its cost, so \begin{equation*}C = 15x + 25y\end{equation*} Next, write the constraints. These will be a system of inequalities. It may help to organize the information into a table. The Healthy Food Store will have \(2x\) kilograms of granola and \(x\) kilograms of muesli from Company A, and \(3y\) kilograms of granola and \(3y\) kilograms of muesli from Company B. The store requires that \begin{align*} 2x + 3y \amp\ge 12\\ x + 3y \amp\ge 9 \end{align*} Because the store cannot purchase negative quantities, we also have \begin{equation*}x \ge 0, ~~~~~y \ge 0\end{equation*}
