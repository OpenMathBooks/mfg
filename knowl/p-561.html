We can also use graphs to solve absolute value inequalities. Look again at the graph of \(y = \abs{3x − 6}\) in Figure 2.113a. Because of the V-shape of the graph, all points with \(y\)-values less than \(9\) lie between the two solutions of \(\abs{3x − 6} = 9\), that is, between \(−1\) and \(5\). Thus, the solutions of the inequality \(\abs{3x − 6} \lt 9\) are \(−1 \lt x \lt 5\). (In the Homework problems, you will be asked to show this algebraically.)
