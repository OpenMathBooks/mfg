A polynomial with exactly two terms, such as \(\frac{1}{2}n^2 + \frac{1}{2}n\), is called a binomial. A polynomial with exactly three terms, such as \(d^2 + 32 d − 21\) or \(128x^3 − 960x^2 + 8000\), is called a trinomial. We have no special names for polynomials with more than three terms.
