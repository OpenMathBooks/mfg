  1. Multiplying a linear equation by a (nonzero) constant does not change its solutions. That is, any solution of the equation \begin{equation*}ax + by = c\end{equation*} is also a solution of the equation \begin{equation*}kax + kby = kc\end{equation*}

  2. Adding (or subtracting) two linear equations does not change their common solutions. That is, any solution of the system \begin{align*} a_1 x + b_1 y \amp = c_1 \\ a_2 x + b_2 y \amp = c_2 \end{align*} is also a solution of the equation \begin{equation*} (a_1 + a_2)x + (b_1 + b_2)y = c_1 + c_2 \end{equation*}
