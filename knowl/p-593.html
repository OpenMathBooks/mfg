The number of bears killed each year by hunters is related to the bear population by the equation \(K = 0.2x\). (Notice that in this model, hunting is adjusted to the size of the bear population.)

  1. Sketch the graphs of \(N\) and \(K\) on the same axes.

  2. When the bear population is \(1200\), which is greater, \(N\) or \(K\)? Will the population increase or decrease in the next year? By how many bears?

  3. When the bear population is \(900\), will the population increase or decrease in the next year? By how many bears?

  4. What sizes of bear population will remain stable after hunting?

  5. What sizes of bear populations will increase despite hunting? What sizes of populations will decrease?

  6. Toward what size will the population tend over time?

  7. Suppose hunting limits are raised so that \(K = 0.3x\). Toward what size will the population tend over time?
