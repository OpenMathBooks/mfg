Because \(t\) represents the time in seconds after the book was dropped, only positive \(t\)-values make sense for the problem. The book stops falling when it hits the ground, at \(h = 0\). You can verify that this happens at approximately \(t = 9.5\) seconds. Thus, only \(t\)-values between \(0\) and \(9.5\) are realistic for this application, so we restrict the domain of the function \(f\) to the interval \([0, 9.5]\). During that time period, the height, \(h\), of the book decreases from \(1454\) feet to \(0\) feet. The range of the function on the domain \([0, 9.5]\) is \([0, 1454]\).
