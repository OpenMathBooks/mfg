The distance from the origin to any point \(P(x, y)\) on the circle is \(r\). Therefore, \begin{equation*}\sqrt{(x − 0)^2 + (y − 0)^2} = r \end{equation*} Or, squaring both sides, \begin{equation*}(x − 0)^2 + (y − 0)^2 = r^2\end{equation*} Thus, the equation for a circle of radius \(r\) centered at the origin is \begin{equation*}x^2 + y^2 = r^2\end{equation*}
