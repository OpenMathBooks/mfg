Semiannually means "twice a year,"" so we use the formula for compound interest with \(P = 1000\), \(r = 0.04\), \(n = 2\), and \(t = 5\). \begin{align*} A(5) \amp = 1000\left(1 + \frac{0.04}{2}\right)^{2(5)} \\ \amp = 1000(1.02)^{10} = 1218.99 \end{align*} If interest is compounded semiannually, the balance in the account after \(5\) years is $\(1218.99\).
