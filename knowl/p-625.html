Many endangered species have fewer than \(1000\) individuals left. To preserve the species, captive breeding programs must maintain a certain effective population, \(N\), given by \begin{equation*}N=\frac{4FM}{F+M} \end{equation*} where \(F\) is the number of breeding females and \(M\) the number of breeding males. (Source: Chapman and Reiss, 1992)

  1. What is the effective population if there are equal numbers of breeding males and females?

  2. In 1972, a breeding program for Speke's gazelle was established with just three female gazelle. Graph the effective population, \(N\), as a function of the number of males.

  3. What is the largest effective population that can be created with three females? How many males are needed to achieve the maximum value?

  4. With three females, for what value of \(M\) is \(N = M\)?

  5. The breeding program for Speke's gazelle began with only one male. What was the effective population?
