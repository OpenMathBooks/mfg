The basic functions \(y = \sqrt{x}\) and \(y = \sqrt[3]{x}\) are power functions of the form \(f (x) = x^{1/n}\), and the graphs of all such functions have shapes similar to those two, depending on whether the index of the root is even or odd. Figure 1.2.17a shows the graphs of \(y = x^{1/2}\), \(y = x^{1/4}\), and \(y = x^{1/6}\). Figure 1.2.17b shows the graphs of \(y = x^{1/3}\), \(y = x^{1/5}\), and \(y = x^{1/7}\).
