  1. Because the point \((−4, 6)\) does lie on the graph, the ordered pair \(x=−4, y = 6\) is a solution of \(y = 0.5x^2 − 2\). We can verify this by substituting \(\alert{-4}\) for \(x\) and \(\blert{6}\) for \(y\): \begin{align*} 0.5(\alert{-4})^2 − 2 \amp= 0.5(16) − 2\\ = 8 − 2 \amp = \blert{6} \end{align*}

  2. Because the point \((3, 0)\) does not lie on the graph, the ordered pair \(x = 3, y = 0\) is not a solution of \(y = 0.5x^2 − 2\). We substitute \(\alert{3}\) for \(x\) and \(\blert{0}\) for \(y\) to verify this. \begin{align*} 0.5(\alert{3})^2 − 2 \amp= 0.5(9) − 2\\ = 4.5 − 2 \amp = 2.5 \ne \blert{0} \end{align*}
