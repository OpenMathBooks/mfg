By using the Pythagorean theorem, we can derive a formula for the distance between two points, \(P_1\) and \(P_2\), in terms of their coordinates. We first label a right triangle, as we did in the example above. Draw a horizontal line through \(P_1\) and a vertical line through \(P_2\). These lines meet at a point \(P_3\), as shown in FigureÂ A.128. The \(x\)-coordinate of \(P_3\) is the same as the \(x\)-coordinate of \(P_2\), and the \(y\)-coordinate of \(P_3\) is the same as the \(y\)-coordinate of \(P_1\). Thus, the coordinates of \(P_3\) are \((x_2, y_1)\).
