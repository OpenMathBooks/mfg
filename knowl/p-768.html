What about \(n\)th roots such as \(\sqrt{23}\) and \(5^{1/3}\) that cannot be evaluated easily? These are examples of irrational numbers. We can use a calculator to obtain decimal approximations to irrational numbers. For example, you can verify that \begin{equation*}\sqrt{23} \approx 4.796 ~ \text{ and } ~ 5^{1/3}\approx 1.710\end{equation*} It is not possible to write down an exact decimal equivalent for an irrational number, but we can find an approximation to as many decimal places as we like.
