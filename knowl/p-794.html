In the preceding examples, exponential growth was modeled by increasing functions of the form \begin{equation*}P(t) = P_0 b^t\end{equation*} where \(b \gt 1\). The function \(P(t) = P_0vb^t\) is a decreasing function if \(0 \lt b \lt 1\). In this case, we say that the function describes exponential decay, and the constant \(b\) is called the decay factor. In Investigation 8, we consider two examples of exponential decay.
