A radical equation is one in which the variable appears under a square root or other radical. The radical may be denoted by a fractional exponent. For example, the equation \begin{equation*}5x^{1/3} = 32\end{equation*} is a radical equation because \(x^{1/3} = \sqrt[3]{x}\). To solve the equation, we first isolate the power to get \begin{equation*}x^{1/3} = 6.4\end{equation*} Then we raise both sides of the equation to the reciprocal of \(\frac{1}{3}\), or 3. \begin{align*} \left(x^{1/3}\right)^3 \amp = 6.4^3 \\ x \amp = 262.144 \end{align*}
