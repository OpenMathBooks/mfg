The same principles apply to powers with fractional exponents. Thus \begin{equation*}(−32)^{1/5} = −2\end{equation*} but \((−64)^{1/6}\) is not a real number. On the other hand, \begin{equation*}−64^{1/6} = −2\end{equation*} because the exponent \(1/6\) applies only to \(64\), and the negative sign is applied after the root is computed.
