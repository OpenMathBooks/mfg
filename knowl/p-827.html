The graph of \(x^2 + (y − 4)^2 = 7\) is a circle with radius \(\sqrt{7} \) and center at \((0, 4)\text{.}\). From the center, move \(\sqrt{7} \text{,}\), or approximately \(2.6\text{,}\), units in each of the four coordinate directions to obtain the points \((0, 6.6)\text{,}\), \((0, 1.4)\text{,}\), \((−2.6, 4)\text{,}\), and \((2.6, 4)\text{.}\). Sketch the circle through these four points. (See Figure A.133b.)
