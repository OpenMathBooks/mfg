Factor numerator and denominator to obtain \begin{equation*}\frac{(x − 6) (x − 1)}{(6 − x) (6 + x)} \end{equation*} The factor \(x-6\) in the numerator is the opposite of the factor \(6-x\) in the denominator. That is, \(x − 6 = −1 (6 − x)\). Thus, \begin{equation*}\frac{−1 \cancel{(6 − x )}(x − 1)}{\cancel{(6 − x )}(6 + x)}=\frac{−1 (x − 1)}{6 + x}=\frac{1-x}{6+x} \end{equation*}

  2. The numerator of the fraction is a difference of two cubes, and the denominator is a difference of two squares. Factor each to obtain \begin{equation*}\frac{\cancel{(3x-1)}(9x^2+3x+1)}{\cancel{(3x-1)}(3x+1)}=\frac{9x^2+3x+1}{3x+1} \end{equation*}
