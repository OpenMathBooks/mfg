We can now solve any exponential equation with base \(10\). For instance, to solve the equation \(16 \cdot 10^t = 360\) we first divide both sides by \(16\) to obtain \begin{equation*}10^t = 22.5\end{equation*} Then we convert the equation to logarithmic form and evaluate: \begin{equation*}t = \log_{10}{22.5} \approx 1.352182518\end{equation*} To \(4\) decimal places, the solution is \(1.3522\).
