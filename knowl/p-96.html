At constant acceleration from rest, the distance traveled by a race car is proportional to the square of the time elapsed. The highest recorded road-tested acceleration is \(0\) to \(60\) miles per hour in \(3.07\) seconds, which produces the following data.

  1. Compute the ratios of the distance traveled to the square of the time elapsed. What was the acceleration, in feet per second squared?

  2. Express the distance traveled, \(d\), as a function of time in seconds, \(t\).

  3. Sketch a graph of the function by hand, and label the scales on the axes.
