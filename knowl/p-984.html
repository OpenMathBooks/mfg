If you wish to receive \(n\) payments per year for \(t\) years from a fund that earns interest rate \(r\) compounded \(n\) times per year, the present value, \(PV\), of the annuity must be \begin{equation*}PV =\frac{P\left[1-\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{-nt}\right]}{\frac{r}{n}}\end{equation*} where each payment is \(P\) dollars.
