The relationship in Example 1.34 holds true for all increasing power and exponential functions: For large enough values of \(x\), the exponential function will always be greater than the power function, regardless of the parameters in the functions. Figure 1.38 shows the graphs of \(f(x) = x^6\) and \(g(x) = 1.8^x\). At first, \(f (x) \gt g(x)\), but at around \(x = 37\), \(g(x)\) overtakes \(f (x)\), and \(g(x) \gt f (x)\) for all \(x \gt 37\).
