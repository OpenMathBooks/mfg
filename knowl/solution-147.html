Solution5.3.20.1

Multiply both sides of the equation by the denominator, \(1 + be^{−kt}\), to get \begin{equation*}P(1 + be^{−kt} ) = a\end{equation*} Then isolate the power, \(e^{−kt}\), as follows: \begin{align*} 1 + be^{−kt} \amp = \frac{a}{P}\amp\amp\text{Subtract 1 from both sides.}\\ be^{−kt} \amp = \frac{a}{P}− 1 \amp\amp\text{Rewrite the right side. }\\ be^{−kt} \amp = \frac{a − P}{P}\amp\amp\text{Divide both sides by }b.\\ e^{−kt} \amp= \frac{a − P}{bP} \end{align*} Take the natural logarithm of both sides to get \begin{equation*}\ln {e^{−kt}} = \ln{\frac{a − P}{bP}}\end{equation*} and recall that \(\ln {e^x} = x\) to simplify the left side. \begin{equation*}−kt = \ln{\frac{a − P}{bP}}\end{equation*} Finally, divide both sides by \(−k\) to solve for \(t\). \begin{equation*}t =\frac{−1}{k}\ln{\frac{a-P}{bP}}\end{equation*}
