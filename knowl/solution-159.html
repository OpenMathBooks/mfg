Solution6.1.1.1
  1. Apply the zero-factor principle to the product \((x − 6)(x + 2)\). There are two solutions, \(6\) and \(−2\). (You should check that both of these values satisfy the original equation.)
  2. To find the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph, we set \(y = 0\) and solve the equation \begin{equation*}0 = x^2 − 4x − 12\end{equation*} But this is the equation we solved in part (a), because \((x − 6)(x + 2) = x^2 − 4x − 12\). The solutions of that equation were \(6\) and \(−2\), so the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph are \(6\) and \(−2\). You can see this by graphing the equation on your calculator, as shown in 6.1.2.
