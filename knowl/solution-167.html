Solution6.25.1
  • Step 1: Write the equation with the constant term on the right side. \begin{equation*}x^2 − 4x{}\text{______}{}=3\end{equation*}
  • Step 2: Now complete the square on the left side. The coefficient of \(x\) is \(−4\), so \begin{equation*}p = \frac{1}{2}(−4) = −2 ~~~\text{ and } ~~~ p^2 = (−2)^2 = 4\end{equation*} We add \(4\) to both sides of our equation: \begin{equation*}x^2 − 4x \alert{{}+4}=3\alert{{}+4}\end{equation*}
  • Step 3: Write the left side as the square of a binomial, and combine terms on the right side: \begin{equation*}(x − 2)^2 =7\end{equation*}
  • Step 4: Finally, use extraction of roots to obtain \begin{align*} x − 2 \amp =\sqrt{7} \amp\text{or}\amp\amp x − 2 \amp= −\sqrt{7}\amp\text{Solve each equation.}\\ x \amp =2+\sqrt{7} \amp\text{or}\amp\amp x \amp=2 −\sqrt{7} \end{align*} The solutions are \(2+\sqrt{7}\approx 4.646\) and \(2−\sqrt{7}\approx −0.646\).
