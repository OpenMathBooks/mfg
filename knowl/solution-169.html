Solution6.30.1

Write the equation in standard form as \begin{equation*}2x^2 − 4x + 1 = 0\end{equation*} Substitute \(2\) for \(a\), \(−4\) for \(b\), and \(1\) for \(c\) into the quadratic formula, then simplify. \begin{align*} x \amp =\frac{−(−4)\pm \sqrt{(−4)^2 − 4(2)(1)}}{2(2)} \\ \amp = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{8}}{4} \end{align*} Using a calculator, we find that the solutions are approximately \(1.707\) and \(0.293\). (If you would like to review following the order of operations on a calculator, please see Appendix B.) We can also verify that the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph of \(y = 2x^2 − 4x + 1\) are approximately \(1.707\) and \(0.293\), as shown in Figure 6.31.
