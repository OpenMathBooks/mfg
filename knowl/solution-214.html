Solution7.3.25.1

To plot \(z = 2 − 2i\), we move from the origin \(2\) units to the right and \(2\) units down. To plot \(\overline{z} = 2 + 2i\), we move from the origin \(2\) units to the right and \(2\) units up. To plot \(−z = −2 + 2i\), we move from the origin \(2\) units to the left and \(2\) units up. To plot \(−\overline{z} = −2 − 2i\), we move from the origin \(2\) units to the left and \(2\) units down. All four points are plotted in Figure 7.3.26.
