Next, we make a table showing x-values close to \(3\), as in Figure 7.73a. As we choose \(x\)-values closer and closer to \(3\), \((x − 3)^2\) gets closer to \(0\), so the fraction \(\frac{2}{(x-3)^2}\) gets very large. This means that the graph approaches, but never touches, the vertical line \(x=3\). In other words, the graph has a vertical asymptote at \(x=3\). We indicate the vertical asymptote by a dashed line, as shown in Figure 7.73b.
