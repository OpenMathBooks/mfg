Solution8.3.3.1

To obtain \(0\) as the first entry in the second row, we can multiply the first row by \(-3\) and add the result to the second row. That is, we add \(−3(\text{row }1)\) to \(\text{row }2\): \begin{equation*} \left[ \begin{array}{@{}cc|c@{}} 1 \amp -4 \amp -5 \\ 3 \amp 6 \amp 3\\ \end{array}\right] \xrightarrow[−3(\text{row } 1) + \text{row }2]{} \left[ \begin{array}{@{}cc|c@{}} 1 \amp -4 \amp -5 \\ 0 \amp 18 \amp 18 \\ \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
