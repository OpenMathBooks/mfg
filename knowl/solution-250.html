Solution8.4.16.1

The last two inequalities, \(x\ge 0\) and \(y\ge 0\), restrict the solutions to the first quadrant. Graph the line \(x − y − 2 = 0\) and use the test point \((0, 0)\) to shade the half-plane, including the origin. Finally, graph the line \(x − 2y − 6 = 0\) and again use the test point \((0, 0)\) to shade the half-plane below the line. The intersection of the shaded regions is shown in Figure 8.4.17.

To find the coordinates of the vertices \(A\), \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\), solve simultaneously the equations of the two lines that intersect at the vertex. Thus,

for \(A\), solve the system

\begin{align*} x \amp= 0\\ y\amp =0 \end{align*}

to find \((0, 0)\)

for \(B\), solve the system

\begin{align*} x \amp= 0\\ x + 2y \amp= 6 \end{align*}

to find \((0, 3)\)

for \(C\), solve the system

\begin{align*} x + 2y \amp= 6\\ x -y \amp= 2 \end{align*}

to find \(\left(\frac{10}{3},\frac{4}{3} \right)\)

for \(D\), solve the system

\begin{align*} y \amp= 0\\ x -y \amp= 2 \end{align*}

to find \(\left(2,0 \right)\)

The vertices are the points \((0, 0)\), \((0, 3)\), \(\left(\frac{10}{3},\frac{4}{3} \right)\), and \((2, 0)\).
