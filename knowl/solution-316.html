From FigureÂ A.11.14, we can show that \(\Delta ABC\) is similar to \(\Delta ADE\). Both triangles include \(\angle A\), and because \(\overline{DE}\) is parallel to \(\overline{BC} \), \(\angle ADE\) is equal to \(\angle ABC\). Thus, the triangles have two pairs of equal angles and are therefore similar triangles.

Step 1

Let \(w\) stand for the width of the loft.

Step 2

First note that if \(FG = 8\), then \(AF = 12\). Because \(\Delta ABC\) is similar to \(\Delta ADE\), the ratios of their corresponding sides (or corresponding altitudes) are equal. In particular, \begin{equation*}\frac{w}{24}=\frac{12}{20} \end{equation*}

Step 3

Solve the proportion for \(w\). Begin by cross-multiplying. \begin{align*} 20w \amp = (12)(24)\amp\amp\text{Apply the fundamental principle.} \\ w \amp = \frac{288}{20}= 14.4\amp\amp\text{Divide by 20.} \end{align*}

Step 4

The floor of the loft will be \(14.4\) feet wide.
