Solution1.177.1

The line crosses the \(y\)-axis at the point \((0, 3200)\text{,}\) so the \(y\)-intercept is \(3200\text{.}\) To calculate the slope of the line, locate another point, say \((20, 6000)\text{,}\) and compute: \begin{align*}m \amp = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{6000 - 3200}{20 - 0}\\ \amp =\frac{2800}{20}= 140 \end{align*} The slope-intercept form of the equation, with \(m = 140\) and \(b = 3200\text{,}\) is \(y = 3200 + 140x\text{.}\)
