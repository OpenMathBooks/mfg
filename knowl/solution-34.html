Solution1.5.21.1
  1. On an income of $15,000, the amount of income over $13,000 is $15,000 − $13,000 = $2000, so you would pay $1500 plus 20% of $2000, or \begin{equation*}T = 1500 + 0.20(2000) = 1900\end{equation*} You can compute the other function values in the same way.
  2. On an income of \(I\), the amount of income over $13,000 is \(I − $13,000\), so you would pay $1500 plus 20% of \(I − 13,000\), or \begin{equation*}T = 1500 + 0.20 (I − 13,000)\end{equation*}
  3. Simplify the right side of the equation to get \begin{align*} T &= 1500 + 0.20I − 2600\\ T &= −1100 + 0.20I \end{align*}
