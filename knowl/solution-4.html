Solution1.13.1

The polynomial has degree six, an even number, so its graph starts at the upper left and extends to the upper right. Its \(y\)-intercept is \begin{equation*}f(0) = (2)^3(−1)(−3)^2 = −72\end{equation*} \(f\) has a zero of multiplicity three at \(x = −2\), a zero of multiplicity one at \(x = 1\), and a zero of multiplicity two at \(x = 3\). The graph has an S-shaped curve at \(x = −2\), crosses the \(x\)-axis at \(x = 1\), touches the \(x\)-axis at \(x = 3\), and then changes direction, as shown in Figure 1.14.
