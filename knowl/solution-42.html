SolutionA.68.1

  1. Find two numbers whose product is \(12\) and whose sum is \(-7\text{.}\) Because the product is positive and the sum is negative, the two numbers must both be negative. The possible factors of \(12\) are \(-1\) and \(-12\text{,}\) \(-2\) and \(-6\text{,}\) or \(-3\) and \(-4\text{.}\) Only \(-4\) and \(-3\) have the correct sum, \(-7\text{.}\) Hence, \begin{equation*}x^2 − 7x + 12 = (x − 4) (x − 3)\end{equation*}

  2. Find two numbers whose product is \(-12\) and whose sum is \(-1\text{.}\) Because the product is negative, the two numbers must be of opposite sign and their sum must be \(-1\text{.}\) By listing the possible factors of \(-12\text{,}\) we find that the two numbers are \(-4\) and \(3\text{.}\) Hence, \begin{equation*}x^2 − x − 12 = (x − 4) (x + 3)\end{equation*}
