  1. The graph of \(f\) is shown in Figure 1.18, with \(t\) on the horizontal axis and \(P\) on the vertical axis.
  2. We solve \(P = 12 + 2t^3\) for \(t\) in terms of \(P\). \begin{align*} 2t^3 \amp = P − 12\amp\amp\text{Substract 12 from both sides.}\\ t^3 \amp = \frac{P − 12}{2}\amp\amp\text{Divide both sides by 2.}\\ t \amp = \sqrt[3]{\frac{P − 12}{2}}\amp\amp\text{Take cube roots.} \end{align*} The inverse function is \(t = f^{−1}(P) =\sqrt[3]{\frac{P − 12}{2}}\). It tells us the number of years it takes for the pheasant population to grow to size \(P\).
  3. The graph of \(f^{−1}\) is shown in Figure 1.19, with \(P\) on the horizontal axis and \(t\) on the vertical axis.
