Solution1.17.1

We first write the equation in standard form as a quadratic equation in the variable \(x\). \begin{equation*}x^2 − yx + (y − 2) = 0\end{equation*} Expressions in \(y\) are treated as constants with respect to \(x\), so that \(a = 1\), \(b = −y\), and \(c = y −2\). Substitute these expressions into the quadratic formula. \begin{align*} x \amp=\frac{−(−y) \pm\sqrt{(−y)^2 − 4(1)(y − 2)}}{2(1)}\\ \amp = \frac{y \pm\sqrt{y^2 − 4y + 8}}{2} \end{align*}
