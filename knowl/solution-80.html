Solution3.35.1

Suppose you weigh \(W\) pounds at distance \(D\) from the center of the Earth. Then \(W = \dfrac{k}{D^2}\text{.}\) At distance \(2D\text{,}\) your weight will be

\begin{equation*} w = \frac{k}{(2D)^2}= \frac{k}{4D^2}= \frac{1}{4}\cdot\frac{k}{D^2} = \frac{1}{4}W \end{equation*}

Your new weight will be \(\frac{1}{4}\) of your old weight. By a similar calculation, you can check that by tripling the distance, your weight will be reduced to \(\frac{1}{9}\) of its original value.
