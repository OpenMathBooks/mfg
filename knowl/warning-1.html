Caution1.9

In Example 1.5, it is essential that we first divide each term of the equation by \(2\), the coefficient of \(x^2\). The following attempt at a solution is incorrect. \begin{align*} 2x^2 − 6x \amp= 5\\ 2x^2 − 6x + 9 \amp= 5 + 9\\ (2x − 3)^2 \amp= 14 ~~~ \rightarrow ~~~ \text{ Incorrect!} \end{align*} You can check that \((2x − 3)^2\) is not equal to \(2x^2 − 6x + 9\). We have not written the left side of the equation as a perfect square, so the solutions we obtain by extracting roots will not be correct.
